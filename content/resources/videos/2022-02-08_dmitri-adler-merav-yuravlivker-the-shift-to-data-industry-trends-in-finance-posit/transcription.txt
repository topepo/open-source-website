<h3>Ethical use of data</h3>

<p><a data-t="2016">So before we finish up the presentation, this is an area that we always like to emphasize both in our training programs and also in our presentations, which is about the ethical use of data.</a> <a data-t="2027">So this is a case study that maybe some of you know.</a> <a data-t="2031">This was one that was done by Target.</a> <a data-t="2036">So essentially about 20 years ago at this point in time, Target had a lot of information.</a> <a data-t="2041">They're a huge retail store in the United States for those who might not live here.</a> <a data-t="2047">And they had a large customer base of data about purchases, past purchases.</a> <a data-t="2054">And wanted to start to predict which of their customers would be pregnant because they know that when an individual is pregnant, they tend to get more set in their habits.</a> <a data-t="2065">And if they can catch somebody during that stage, they tend to be more loyal customers throughout their life.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2072">So in order to do that, they had their data scientists sit for about two to multiple years of data to better understand, OK, which of their customers became pregnant.</a> <a data-t="2083">And then what were the key factors there that they can use to then predict who will become pregnant?</a> <a data-t="2089">So they did this analysis.</a> <a data-t="2091">And at the end of this analysis, they found some key indicators included buying ginger ale to help with nausea.</a> <a data-t="2098">Stopped buying wine was one of the factors that had a big correlation, as well as buying prenatal vitamins and things like that.</a> <a data-t="2108">And based on their predictions of who would become pregnant, they started to send out targeted flyers.</a> <a data-t="2113">And you'd think this is a great use case of data analytics and it is a demonstration of an effective use case.</a> <a data-t="2120">But what ended up happening is they sent one of these flyers to the parents of a 15 year old who hadn't told them that she was pregnant.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2127">So that became a big case that happened with a few other families as well.</a> <a data-t="2133">And it started to bring up the question of it's one thing to be able to mine customer data for insights.</a> <a data-t="2139">But how can you make sure you're using it in a way that doesn't cause harm, doesn't have ethical implications to it?</a> <a data-t="2148">So it's important, especially given that we're in the finance space, to think about how we're using customer data and how we need to be mindful of that fact.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2157">Now, interestingly enough, what Target ended up doing, it's not that they don't use that data for insights anymore.</a> <a data-t="2162">What happens is that they start to put lawnmowers next to baby cribs.</a> <a data-t="2167">So they just are a little bit more subtle about how they advertise that in the future.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2173">Speaking about ethical considerations, another one that's popped up, especially in the past few years, is about biases that exist in our current data that inform our models.</a> <a data-t="2185">So, again, it's our responsibility to ask these types of questions.</a> <a data-t="2189">There's one where it's, you know, how does the data reflect the society that we're in today?</a> <a data-t="2194">And if it does perpetuate any stereotypes or biases, how can we build models for future prediction or, for example, granting loans that will mitigate some of those biases and risks so that we don't perpetuate that?</a> <a data-t="2209">This is a difficult question.

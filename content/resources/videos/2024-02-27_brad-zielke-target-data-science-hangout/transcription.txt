<h3>Understanding inputs, outputs, and asking why</h3>

<p><a data-t="2012">I'll start probably even more simplistic.</a> <a data-t="2015">Like, I think the biggest thing I preach to most of my business partners and even team is, do you understand the inputs that are affecting your outputs?</a> <a data-t="2027">And do you really understand what went into those decisions or those variables that led to, you know, the other models or algorithms or all of that?</a> <a data-t="2038">And most of the time, what I find is a lot of hypotheses on what happened within those inputs.</a> <a data-t="2043">Well, somebody else owned that or somebody else knew that or that was already decided and I can't change it.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2051">And in reality, there's so much of just, can you affect the inputs instead of just changing or overriding or, you know, biasing in between what you want to have happen?</a> <a data-t="2062">That I think it starts with, do you take the time to understand those effects of what happened upstream of you?</a> <a data-t="2068">Which regardless of science, I think is a really good behavior.</a> <a data-t="2075">If I start with just what has happened before I ended up having to make this decision or what I'm dealing with right now, I can usually start with a point of empathy to say, what did they deal with?</a> <a data-t="2087">What was constrained for them? What were variables that they weren't able to consider? What was the data they didn't have?</a> <a data-t="2093">And then I can work back even to their inputs and say, wow, if I actually change this other thing or help them understand that other thing, the effects all the way through this can be changed as well.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2108">I would just ask, you know, why? Why is this a problem?</a> <a data-t="2113">Like, you know, I think asking that why and understanding it, there's oftentimes where people will come to us with problems like, we need data science to do this.</a> <a data-t="2129">And when you ask why and then you ask them to define it, sometimes it's not even a data science problem, right?</a> <a data-t="2135">Like you need a binary decision, you know, and sometimes like we can help you build that, like we can do that.</a> <a data-t="2143">But like that doesn't take any data science model.</a> <a data-t="2146">And so I think asking the why and then digging into like, what do you really want to accomplish? Sometimes you can really simplify the solution without even having to do the work.</a></p>

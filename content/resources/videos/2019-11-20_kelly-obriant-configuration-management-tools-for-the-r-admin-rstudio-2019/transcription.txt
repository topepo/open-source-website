<h3>The R admin's role in legitimizing R</h3>

<p><a data-t="102">As part of this overarching set of goals that we have for R admins, a big part of doing the work of an R admin is making R legitimate in your organizations.</a> <a data-t="116">The R admin looks at their enterprise analytic infrastructure on a wider scale than maybe a traditional data scientist would.</a> <a data-t="133">They are aware of all of the different integration points and all of the places where R can be assisting in helping manage that infrastructure.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="157">So we kind of think about these things in three different parts.</a> <a data-t="161">Your first step is to get R recognized as legitimate.</a> <a data-t="166">If you are in the back room and IT is giving you the secret server and you've installed R on it and you're kind of doing things in a shady way and giving people access to different things, like, we understand that that's going on, but we're trying to give you a framework to move out of the shadows and bring R into the light.</a> <a data-t="166">So in order to do that, we say it's important for you, the R admin, to develop competencies in using our professional stack.</a> <a data-t="179">So in order to do that, you need to get hands-on experience with understanding and managing R tooling.</a> <a data-t="201">And finally, you need to take the step to communicate with your IT department and get R adopted and finally, hopefully, help others in your organization so that they all get to the point where various teams are all relying on integrated R-based solutions.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="221">So that's the ideal.</a> <a data-t="222">But we understand that this is kind of an open-ended framework, and it can be kind of frustrating and difficult to navigate because we don't have a lot of actionable steps listed here.</a> <a data-t="233">One of my friends, Adam, recently, in the past couple of months, started this thread on Twitter that was really, really interesting where he reached out to the Rstats community and said, hey, does anyone have any resources for starting conversations with IT?</a> <a data-t="250">You know, I'm struggling to make R an accepted part of our software kit.</a> <a data-t="255">And I know Adam, and he is a very talented, capable R admin, but he's feeling stuck.</a></p>

<h3>Radio 2020 and the noise of digital life</h3>

<p><a data-t="250">So after being pinged all morning and exposed to the stressful world of 2020 during</a> <a data-t="256">COVID and the amplification of that world in my digital media, I start hearing not the individual</a> <a data-t="262">noise, but the steady stream of Radio KFKD. <p><a data-t="305">Today we're living in Radio KFKD, but over top of</a> <a data-t="311">that is the pandemic. So it's what I like to call Radio 2020. <p><a data-t="349">Being isolated is hard enough as a human. <p><a data-t="387">Why is radio 2020 so good at derailing us?</a> <a data-t="393">And why is it constantly creating noise for us? There's three reasons. <p><a data-t="453">Sometimes this engagement is good, like if we're discovering interesting blog posts, or donating</a> <a data-t="459">to creators, or watching original content. But oftentimes it can simply be noise. The online</a> <a data-t="465">noise interferes with our ability to concentrate on longer-term projects and engage in longer-term</a> <a data-t="471">thinking. <p><a data-t="478">A second problem with this content is a lot of it is very superlative.</a> <a data-t="483">What do I mean when I say superlative? <p><a data-t="537">The third problem is that online is worse in the context of the current</a> <a data-t="543">state of the internet. <h3>Building your own digital garden</h3>

<p><a data-t="578">So we have this crisis that's occurring both in the physical world, with everything that's</a> <a data-t="584">been happening during the pandemic, and in the digital world with a crisis for our attention as well.</a> <a data-t="590">How do we fight this? <p><a data-t="607">Anne Lamott in Bird by Bird says that the best thing to do is to take it piece by piece, and the</a> <a data-t="614">title of the book comes from a book report that her brother had to write. 30 years ago, she writes,</a> <a data-t="620">my older brother, who was 10 years old at the time, was trying to get a report on birds that he'd</a> <a data-t="627">written that he had three months to write. <p><a data-t="650">The idea is that we can't change everything, but even changing a little tiny bit of our environment</a> <a data-t="656">is better than changing nothing. <p><a data-t="666">The Secret Garden is a book</a> <a data-t="672">by Frances Hogson Burnett, who lived in America and England in the 19th century. <p><a data-t="735">So right now, we're all a little</a> <a data-t="741">bit like Mary, at the start of a garden that's fallow. We're lost. We're not quite sure what to</a> <a data-t="747">do. <h3>Ways people have cultivated their digital gardens</h3>

<p><a data-t="765">So how do we fix this? How do we create something that's for our public garden</a> <a data-t="771">and makes the internet better at the same time? <p><a data-t="785">The first and best way, in my opinion,</a> <a data-t="791">is to write something from your perspective. <p><a data-t="835">You can write about anything from</a> <a data-t="840">analytics, from your opinion on certain trends, certain packages you've done.</a> <a data-t="846">So these topics have been written about hundreds of thousands of times before data analysis</a> <a data-t="853">and </a><a href="/software/tidyverse/">tidyverse</a><a data-t="853">, but the important thing is that it's coming from your perspective. <p><a data-t="968">Another place I've been writing is my newsletter, Normcore Tech, where I look at machine learning</a> <a data-t="973">and data, but more from a human-centered perspective. <h3>Smaller ways to get started</h3>

<p><a data-t="1185">But what if you're overwhelmed by</a> <a data-t="1190">the idea of getting started and don't know how?</a> <a data-t="1196">There's a lot of really small ideas that you can draw from as well, that don't necessarily involve</a> <a data-t="1202">creating a course or making videos or creating your own blog.

<h3>What data scientists should do more and less</h3>

<p><a data-t="3681">Yeah, so I would say for data practitioners in general, and this goes for analysts and scientists, when I was managing the team at Immuta, one thing I found myself saying to our data scientists a lot was, like, how can we simplify, like, simplify, simplify, like, things, because in the business context, and again, we're a smaller company, we weren't building, like, and we weren't building, like, models, right, that had to, like, accommodate a bunch of stuff.</a> <a data-t="3728">We were building more of just data products, and dashboards, and scores, and things like that for our business consumers.</a></p>

<h3>Building trust in data and getting people to feel ownership</h3>

<p><a data-t="4021">What I found is that the more you can do that, the more people can, like, feel, feel ownership over it.</a> <a data-t="4021">We did this one thing at Immuta where we exposed Salesforce, which is our customer relationship management platform.</a> <a data-t="4046">It has, like, all of our customer lists and stuff in it.</a> <a data-t="4046">Salesforce data comes into the warehouse, then it gets exposed in our BI tool Looker.</a> <a data-t="4057">And there was this weird thing where the person who managed Salesforce would look at numbers in Looker and they would be, like, I don't know what these are.</a> <a data-t="4064">Like, they wouldn't, they didn't feel ownership over it simply because it was, like, going through our pipes.</a> <a data-t="4076">And so we re-architected things and tried to keep it much more, like, domain oriented.</a> <a data-t="4085">And they, that had the positive effect of, like, making them feel more ownership over it.</a> <a data-t="4089">Because they would go and they'd look at Looker and they'd look at Salesforce and be like, okay, this matches up.</a></p>

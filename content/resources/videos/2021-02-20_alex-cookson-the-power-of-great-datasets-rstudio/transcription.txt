<h3>What makes a great dataset</h3>

<p><a data-t="57">Now when I say great datasets, I'm talking about something pretty specific.</a> <a data-t="61">I'm talking about datasets that are both cool and interesting.</a> <a data-t="65">And when I say cool, I mean something that when you come across it, you say to yourself,</a> <a data-t="69">hey, that's cool, or that's really fun.</a> <a data-t="72">It's something that speaks to you on a deeper level, and it tends to be pretty personal.</a> <a data-t="77">So what's cool to you might not be cool to me.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="80">And when I say interesting, I mean that it rouses your curiosity.</a> <a data-t="84">It makes you start asking a whole bunch of questions that you really want the answer to.</a> <a data-t="89">If you've ever played a video game where you say to yourself just five more minutes or</a> <a data-t="92">one more turn, and you're still playing it an hour later, this is the dataset version of that.</a> <a data-t="97">Just one more graph.</a></p>

<h3>Examples of great datasets</h3>

<p><a data-t="100">So some examples that I enjoy are the Duke Lemur Center dataset, which looks at ages</a> <a data-t="107">and weights of almost 30 different species of lemur.</a> <a data-t="111">The Broadway Grosses dataset, which looks at weekly box office grosses, tickets sold,</a> <a data-t="118">average top ticket prices of Broadway shows going back to 1985.</a> <a data-t="124">And fictional character personalities, which I consider to be one of the all-time greatest</a> <a data-t="128">of great datasets.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="130">And this one looks at personality traits of 800 fictional characters across over 250 different</a> <a data-t="135">spectrums, such as the playful to serious spectrum, where we might find Michael Scott</a> <a data-t="141">from The Office on the playful side and Worf from Star Trek Next Generation way over on</a> <a data-t="146">the serious side.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="148">And I think this is such a great dataset because I've been watching so much TV and so many</a> <a data-t="152">movies lately that when I came across it, I thought, oh, this is so fun.</a> <a data-t="156">It's something that speaks to something I've been spending a lot of my time doing.</a> <a data-t="160">And I immediately had tons and tons of questions that I wanted to ask it and uncover from this</a> <a data-t="166">dataset.</a></p>

<h3>Learning R through great datasets</h3>

<p><a data-t="167">One of those questions was, what are some of my favorite characters' personalities?</a> <a data-t="171">And I put in a whole bunch of work and ended up with some graphs that looked like this,</a> <a data-t="175">which takes the six characters from Pride and Prejudice and shows their strongest traits.</a> <a data-t="181">So we see that Elizabeth Bennett at the top left is a treasure as opposed to trash.</a> <a data-t="186">She's important.</a> <a data-t="187">She has a high IQ.</a> <a data-t="188">She's independent and beautiful and a feminist.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="192">But to put this together, I had to learn to do a whole bunch of things I didn't know</a> <a data-t="196">how to do before, like use custom fonts so I could get this Georgian era kind of handwriting</a> <a data-t="202">vibe, to tweak labels using the glue and ggtext packages.</a> <a data-t="207">I had to choose an appropriate color palette for the subject matter and adjust a ton of</a> <a data-t="211">theme elements using </a><a href="/software/ggplot2/">ggplot2</a><a data-t="211">.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="214">A second question I had was, which characters are most similar to or different than one another?</a> <a data-t="219">And again, I put in a bunch of work and ended up with something like this, which looks at</a> <a data-t="224">reasonable versus deranged characters plotted against rugged versus refined characters.</a> <a data-t="229">So here we see that characters like Sandra Clegane and Jane Cobb are really similar to one another.</a> <a data-t="234">They're deranged and rugged.</a> <a data-t="236">And their opposites are Charlotte York from Sex and the City and Annie Edison, who are</a> <a data-t="240">reasonable and refined.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="242">And to do this, I had to conduct principal component analysis with tidy models because</a> <a data-t="248">these are actually principal components.</a> <a data-t="250">You don't find them in the data.</a> <a data-t="251">I had to learn to add text and arrow annotations to get this graph here.</a> <a data-t="255">And I wrote a blog post where this is an interactive chart.</a> <a data-t="259">So I had to learn how to make an interactive chart using Plotly.</a></p>

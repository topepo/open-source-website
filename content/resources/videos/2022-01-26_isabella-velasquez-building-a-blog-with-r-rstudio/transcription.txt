<h3>What to consider before starting</h3>

<p><a data-t="841">And so in addition, what else do you need to consider if you want to write an internal blog?</a> <a data-t="849">I think first and foremost is why do you want to create an internal blog, and what are your goals</a> <a data-t="854">and your ultimate purpose?</a> <a data-t="854">Are you hoping to get viewership for just your team or outside of your</a> <a data-t="861">team or others at the organization and hope that they are interested in the work as well?</a> <a data-t="868">Are you hoping that the blog stays focused about work projects, or is it more of an opportunity</a> <a data-t="875">to learn more about the personal experiences of others?</a> <a data-t="882">And so having a clear goal can help you</a> <a data-t="882">develop your blog, what it looks like, and help you explain to others its goal and purposes as well.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="888">And related to your goals, I think it's very important to illustrate the values that you want</a> <a data-t="894">to instill.</a> <a data-t="904">So if your blog is meant to be a place for inclusivity, openness, a way to motivate your</a> <a data-t="904">co-workers, then you want to make sure that the content of your blog demonstrates your values</a> <a data-t="909">and that your colleagues are aware of these values as well.</a> <a data-t="912">And another thing to consider is, again, what would be helpful and relevant content?</a> <a data-t="918">What do you want to share that your readers will actually want to read?</a> <a data-t="924">And for an internal data</a> <a data-t="924">science community, what would be interesting enough to check in periodically at your blog to</a> <a data-t="931">learn more for your teammates?</a> <a data-t="938">A key to success is knowing what others find valuable and making</a> <a data-t="944">sure that your blog reflects</a> <a data-t="944">that information.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="953">And like we mentioned before, we don't want a blog to confuse others because</a> <a data-t="953">of the other forms of documentation that already exist within an organization.</a> <a data-t="959">So it's important</a> <a data-t="959">to know what's in and out of your blog, the tone, style, and voice that it will have,</a> <a data-t="967">and what sort of content are within the guardrails of your blog and what is not.</a> <a data-t="972">Of course, as time goes on and you learn things, things may change,</a> <a data-t="976">but having these in place can help you understand how you think you'll meet your goals</a> <a data-t="981">and adapt accordingly.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="989">And ideally, you can get your colleagues involved in content creation as</a> <a data-t="989">well.</a> <a data-t="994">This way you can showcase many different voices and perspectives and keep your blog</a> <a data-t="994">updated with diverse ideas.</a> <a data-t="1001">Of course, this means your colleagues must be excited to be</a> <a data-t="1001">involved and share their own skill sets.</a> <a data-t="1006">And this can be done through direct invitations,</a> <a data-t="1006">but it is helpful if you have firm values and great content for your blog</a> <a data-t="1011">to help encourage collaboration.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1018">And then another thing to note is that you</a> <a data-t="1018">want to get buy-in for your blog, not just from your teammates, but also from your leadership team</a> <a data-t="1023">since it takes time to create content.</a> <a data-t="1029">You might have to make the case that it's worth the time and</a> <a data-t="1029">effort of not just you, but also your colleagues to keep a frequently updated blog.</a> <a data-t="1036">And this is</a> <a data-t="1036">done through the creation of valuable content, work that makes it interesting and useful and</a> <a data-t="1041">applicable to others, and being able to communicate the value of that work to higher-ups.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1060">And so, like, if your blog's values are to be inclusive, you don't want to unintentionally</a> <a data-t="1069">exclude folks with the topics or the way that folks respond to discussions.</a> <a data-t="1074">So, getting consistent feedback and help can help determine whether your blog</a> <a data-t="1081">is truly benefiting your team and reflecting the values that you want to put in place.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1087">And related to ownership, you want the blog to be a part of the routine of your work so that it</a> <a data-t="1093">doesn't feel like a big imposition or burden on you or others.</a> <a data-t="1101">Perhaps your co-workers have given</a> <a data-t="1101">a conference talk, or you just had a big strategy meeting.</a> <a data-t="1108">Can a blog post just be part of the workflow</a> <a data-t="1108">so that the blog has regular content, but it's not a creation of a whole new thing?</a> <a data-t="1115">And if it's already somewhat integrated, then it's a lot easier to keep the blog going.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1121">And then, finally, there is the technical stuff, too.</a> <a data-t="1129">So, there's the actual tools that you'll be</a> <a data-t="1129">using to build your blog, and the training around those tools.</a> <a data-t="1135">You don't want something like a blog</a> <a data-t="1135">to disappear when a particular person, you know, moves to another team or something like that.</a> <a data-t="1147">And hopefully, you can build the skills across your team to make sure that your blog doesn't</a> <a data-t="1147">disappear.</a> <a data-t="1151">And hopefully, you can build the skills across your team,</a> <a data-t="1151">the infrastructure across your organization to keep it going.</a></p>

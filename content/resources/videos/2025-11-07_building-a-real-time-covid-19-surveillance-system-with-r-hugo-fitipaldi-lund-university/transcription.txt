<p><a data-t="1">The COVID Symptom Study was a project that used an app that was developed by the health data science company ZOE in the UK,</a> <a data-t="10">and it was launched in the UK together with King's College London.</a> <a data-t="14">The app was also launched subsequently in the US with the help of Harvard University,</a> <a data-t="21">and in Sweden with us from Lund University and our friends from Uppsala University.</a> <a data-t="26">This app was launched very early in the pandemic in Europe and in the US,</a> <a data-t="32">so early March for the UK and the US, but also in April for Sweden.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="141">From that day on, the participants would be reminded to report once a day at least about how they were feeling that day,</a> <a data-t="152">if they were feeling healthy or if they were feeling unhealthy.</a> <a data-t="155">If the participants said that they were not feeling healthy,</a> <a data-t="158">then we would ask them with a list of symptoms to point us which symptoms they were having</a> <a data-t="164">or to write in a text field which symptoms that they were having.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="169">We also asked participants that at the time had availability of COVID tests,</a> <a data-t="177">since as I said, this was very early in the pandemic,</a> <a data-t="181">COVID tests were not available for everyone.</a> <a data-t="185">Most groups that were at risk that we identified in the beginning,</a> <a data-t="189">but also health workers were able to have these tests.</a> <a data-t="196">So we asked for anyone in our app that had information about tests in terms of,</a> <a data-t="201">did you take a test?

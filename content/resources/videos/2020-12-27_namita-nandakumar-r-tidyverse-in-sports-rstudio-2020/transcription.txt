<h3>Building the model</h3>

<p><a data-t="664">The first thing we can do as kind of a really lazy baseline thing is just look at distance because I feel like that's something that's really important.</a> <a data-t="672">And we can just use a logistic regression with a polynomial term and just estimate the probability of scoring a goal based on the absolute distance to the net.</a> <a data-t="683">So the X axis there is shot distance.</a> <a data-t="685">The Y axis is the probability.</a> <a data-t="688">And we can see that just based on this one feature, your estimate can be anywhere from near 0% to near upwards of 20% if you're really close to the net.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="701">The slightly less lazy thing to do would be to use something like XGBoost and also incorporate shot angle, shot type, time and distance from the last event.</a> <a data-t="712">And also not assume linearity.</a> <a data-t="715">We can do some cross validation to choose the number of boosting rounds.</a> <a data-t="720">And I always feel like whenever I talk about machine learning, I make it sound really simple and unimpressive.</a> <a data-t="724">And I know you're not supposed to do that, which is why I've incorporated these images from Google searches of machine learning of like big brains and lots of nodes and stuff.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="730">And when we're talking about variable inclusion, it's worth really thinking about it.</a> <a data-t="736">Because frankly, we're not working with big data here.</a> <a data-t="742">There's not that many shots even when you're going over multiple seasons.</a> <a data-t="747">And so I don't think this is the type of problem that requires like a kitchen sink approach of just like tossing in all the variables and seeing what sticks.</a> <a data-t="757">I think it's worth it to kind of rely on some domain knowledge as well and think about what should be important, things like shot distance, shot angle, and what might not be as important.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="763">So I'm not including like score state.</a> <a data-t="766">Because I can't think of a reason why after adjusting for everything else we already know about a shot, like the distance, angle, type, et cetera, I can't think of a compelling reason why it would matter that you took that shot if you were up two or if you were down three or whatever else.</a> <a data-t="793">And fortunately, this has also been confirmed by previous expected goals research.</a> <a data-t="800">You know, researchers that have included score state have found that it's like not really that important.</a> <a data-t="806">So it's good to hear that confirmation.</a></p>

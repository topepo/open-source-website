<h3>The problem of surgery cancellation</h3>

<p><a data-t="46">So the backstory is that in 2011, I came from London to Cincinnati to learn about quality improvement in healthcare at Cincinnati Children's Hospital, which is renowned for that kind of work.</a> <a data-t="59">And they asked me to work on surgery cancellation.</a> <a data-t="66">Immediately, I could find out that about 5% of procedures were cancelled on the day of surgery.</a> <a data-t="72">And this actually equated to about 5% of operating time as well, which is important because a lot of the billing is based on time.</a> <a data-t="78">So through this, we found out that the problem of surgery cancellation probably cost about $100,000 a week in terms of potential lost revenue, which is a lot of money.</a> <a data-t="91">But just taking a more human side to it, it was really difficult and frustrating for families and for staff as well.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="98">So I'd just like to take a moment to put surgery cancellation in terms of some dimensions of quality, which is what's from the Institute of Medicine, which is what people often talk about in healthcare quality.</a> <a data-t="110">Even I didn't know until I came across this paper that about 30% of the global burden of disease needs surgery or other procedures under anaesthesia, either for working it up or for actually treating.</a> <a data-t="120">So clearly, if surgery is cancelled, the effectiveness of that procedure is not going to be realised.</a> <a data-t="126">I touched already on family-centredness, but I'd like to just mention a paper from more than 20 years ago from another major children's hospital.</a> <a data-t="137">They found out that many families drive tens or even hundreds of miles to get to the local children's hospital.</a> <a data-t="143">They often have to pay for accommodation.</a> <a data-t="146">They have to get childcare for their other children.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="149">Mostly, they're taking time off work and more often than not, that's unpaid.</a> <a data-t="155">So when the surgery is cancelled, it's probably not surprising that they're quite emotional and even angry on many occasions.</a> <a data-t="163">Timeliness, clearly, if the surgery doesn't go ahead and has to be rescheduled, then it's not being provided in a timely fashion.</a> <a data-t="173">Efficiency, I already touched on that, and the other one that they always talk about is equitability, and at the point when we started this, we really didn't know what the impact was.</a></p>

<h3>Why children's surgery gets cancelled</h3>

<p><a data-t="183">So why does children's surgery get cancelled? The number one cause is patient illness.</a> <a data-t="189">So with the kids, it's usually coughs, colds, diarrhoea, vomiting, things that give you a fever, which means it's not safe to go ahead with the procedure.</a> <a data-t="200">The second cause is no-show.</a> <a data-t="200">They don't turn up.</a> <a data-t="203">The third most frequent we call NPO violation.</a> <a data-t="207">So in order to have safe anaesthesia, you need to have an empty stomach before we begin, and so we give instructions about when they can safely eat and drink and when they have to stop, and if those are not followed, then we can't go ahead with the procedure.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="225">So we did a few cross-the-board interventions to try and reduce our surgery cancellation rate.</a> <a data-t="230">We came up with some new, more colourful instructions for families with some pictures on of when it was okay to eat and what particular things were okay at particular times, when to show up.</a> <a data-t="242">We sent out some text message reminders.</a> <a data-t="244">And finally, we set up a process that if the pre-op nurses having a phone call a couple of days beforehand had any inkling that the kid was sick, then they would have a process to discuss it with our nurse practitioners and therefore try and avoid cancellation on the day by rescheduling in advance.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="269">So we tested all these interventions, and then we rolled them out across the hospital.</a> <a data-t="276">We were pretty happy that we succeeded in saving about over an hour a day, which was about 17% of the time, and that was good.</a> <a data-t="283">But I'm a perfectionist, and so I wanted to work out why people were still cancelling.</a> <a data-t="288">And also really being curious about this no-show, the second most common.</a> <a data-t="293">So these were people that families didn't arrive, they didn't answer the phone when we tried to get hold of them, so we just really didn't know what was going on.</a></p>

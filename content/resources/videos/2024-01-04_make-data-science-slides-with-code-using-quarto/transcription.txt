<p><a data-t="0">Here's a PSA to data scientists and developers.</a> <a data-t="2">Do you ever need to get your code-based analytics into slides?</a> <a data-t="5">Then definitely check out </a><a href="/software/quarto/">Quarto</a><a data-t="5">.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="7">Quarto is an open-source tool for technical publishing.</a> <a data-t="10">It lets you combine code, output, and narrative,</a> <a data-t="12">say from an exploratory analysis script or your Jupyter Notebook or R Markdown doc,</a> <a data-t="16">and from that, publish all sorts of output, including presentations.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="21">You can convert your work into a lovely set of slides.</a> <a data-t="24">There's nice support for PowerPoint as well as Beamer or LaTeX slides,</a> <a data-t="28">but let me show you how it works with a Reveal.js example.</a></p>

<h3>Creating slides with Quarto</h3>

<p><a data-t="31">You just add a little bit of YAML at the top of your doc</a> <a data-t="33">to tell Quarto how to publish your slides,</a> <a data-t="36">and you separate your slides using Markdown headers.</a> <a data-t="38">There's then a ton of options to customize your slides</a> <a data-t="41">and say and show exactly what you want.</a> <a data-t="43">In part two, I'll run through a bunch of your options.</a></p>

<h3>Introducing the Kyber package</h3>

<p><a data-t="93">Kyber package replaces manual steps with R functions and was driven by the question</a> <a data-t="98">how much automation can we drive with minimal coding for the end-user?</a> <a data-t="102">It's inherently powerful to design around commonly used and widely understood tools.</a> <a data-t="107">So why can't we just do things with a spreadsheet, an R script, and a friendly front door?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="113">Speaking of friendly front doors, Kyber was developed by Sean Cross who is a hero in the R community.</a> <a data-t="120">He was there in the first OpenScapes champions cohort back in 2019 when he got to see</a> <a data-t="126">firsthand the manual effort involved in putting this infrastructure together.</a> <a data-t="129">He developed Kyber in collaboration with Julie Lowndes, OpenScapes founding director, and now Andy Tuescher and</a> <a data-t="136">Anna Holder and Nick Tierney and I have all contributed to Kyber in different ways.</a></p>

<h3>Creating agendas with Kyber</h3>

<p><a data-t="141">So I'll give you one use case, creating an agenda for one of our cohort calls.</a> <a data-t="145">Every agenda has the same basic structure and we use Kyber to make us agenda sandwiches</a> <a data-t="151">from a variety of different recipes and our well-stocked </a><a href="/software/rmarkdown/">RMarkdown</a><a data-t="151"> refrigerator of content.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="158">We put all the metadata for individual cohorts in a Google Sheet.</a> <a data-t="162">So there's the cohort name, a start date, end date, and we put all of the content in</a> <a data-t="168">markdown files as the content that goes into the agendas. In our markdown files, they live on GitHub,</a> <a data-t="174">but they're indexed in the same spreadsheet.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="177">With a single call from Kyber, I give input that is the registry, the URL of the</a> <a data-t="184">Google Sheet, the unique cohort name, and the call number of the agenda I want to create.</a> <a data-t="189">I take the output, I paste it into a Google Doc, and I have my multi-page agenda.</a></p>

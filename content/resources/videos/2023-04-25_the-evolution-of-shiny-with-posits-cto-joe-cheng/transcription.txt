<h3>Origins of <a href="/software/shiny-r/">Shiny</a></h3>

<p><a data-t="12">Shiny really started with a request from one of our users, one of our earliest users of </a><a href="/software/rstudio/">RStudio</a><a data-t="12"> IDE.</a> <a data-t="19">Professor Danny Kaplan asked us for an interactive way to present statistical and math concepts to students.</a> <a data-t="28">And he knew R really well, so he wanted to do it with R.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="31">So it wasn't until probably a year after he had made this request that I stumbled onto a technology called Meteor</a> <a data-t="39">that was pushing this new, to me very alien, form of programming called reactive programming.</a> <a data-t="48">And it took me a while to get my head around how it actually worked and a little digging through their source code.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="55">But finally, after I understood what they were doing, and some time had passed where I guess my brain had marinated it,</a> <a data-t="63">I woke up one morning and I just had this vision of HTML with no JavaScript in it,</a> <a data-t="72">just simple markup that had indicators of what elements were inputs and what elements were outputs.</a> <a data-t="79">Combined that with reactive code written in R that would power that HTML interface</a> <a data-t="88">and tie it all together with a </a><a href="/software/websocket/">WebSocket</a><a data-t="88"> connection.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="91">It really felt like this could be a way to build applications that were interactive enough to satisfy the needs of</a> <a data-t="99">not just Danny, but a lot of Dannys out there in the R world.</a></p>

<blockquote><a data-t="91">It really felt like this could be a way to build applications that were interactive enough to satisfy the needs of</a> <a data-t="99">not just Danny, but a lot of Dannys out there in the R world.</a></blockquote>

<h3><a href="/software/shiny-python/">Shiny for Python</a></h3>

<p><a data-t="104">And I think Shiny will always find its purest expression in R just because of how flexible the language is.</a> <a data-t="112">But it's impossible to ignore how many people could benefit if we bring those same ideas to Python as well.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="124">The design of the language really prioritizes ease of understanding and ease of reading.</a> <a data-t="130">Whereas I think in R we tend to favor concise expression of our ideas and maybe a certain elegance and symmetry.</a> <a data-t="141">And in Python, readability, understandability, simplicity, those are valued more.</a> <a data-t="150">So I think for some people, Python is just home.</a> <a data-t="154">I mean, it's famously the second best language for anything.</a> <a data-t="160">I think that's a label that Pythonistas probably wear with pride.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="166">And because Python is the second best language for anything, it means that if we want to reach more people with Shiny,</a> <a data-t="175">we can reach all those people for whom Python is useful. And that's a lot of people.</a></p>

<blockquote><a data-t="166">And because Python is the second best language for anything, it means that if we want to reach more people with Shiny,</a> <a data-t="175">we can reach all those people for whom Python is useful. And that's a lot of people.</a></blockquote>

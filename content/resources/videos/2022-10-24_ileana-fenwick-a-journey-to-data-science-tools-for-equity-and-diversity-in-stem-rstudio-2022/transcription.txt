<h3>Defining open science and OpenScapes</h3>

<p><a data-t="162">And so, I may have said two words already that you're unfamiliar with, so I want to</a> <a data-t="166">define them quickly.</a> <a data-t="168">The first is open science, and the second is OpenScapes.</a> <a data-t="173">So open science is the tools and the practices that enable all of us to have more reproducible,</a> <a data-t="180">transparent, and inclusive practices for data-intensive science.</a> <a data-t="184">And so, when we're talking about tools, we're talking about these open source softwares</a> <a data-t="189">and practices that we're using, for example, GitHub, and we talk about the practices as</a> <a data-t="193">well and the communities that exist around these tools.</a> <a data-t="196">For example, the RStudio community.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="198">And if you had the pleasure of hearing this morning's keynote, you learned a little bit</a> <a data-t="202">about OpenScapes.</a> <a data-t="204">And so, OpenScapes helps researchers reimagine data-intensive science and also to develop</a> <a data-t="209">the skills to cultivate collaborative and inclusive teams.</a> <a data-t="213">And they do that in two ways in particular.</a> <a data-t="216">The first is through the OpenScapes Champions program, which I was a participant in, which</a> <a data-t="221">is an open science mentorship program for science teams.</a> <a data-t="225">And the second is through cultivating the OpenScapes mindset, which is a community-building</a> <a data-t="230">framework to support the transition to open science.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="234">And it relies on these three principles that turn into a cycle of community building.</a> <a data-t="238">The first is to empower research teams, the second is to amplify leaders, and the third</a> <a data-t="244">is to engage mentors.</a></p>

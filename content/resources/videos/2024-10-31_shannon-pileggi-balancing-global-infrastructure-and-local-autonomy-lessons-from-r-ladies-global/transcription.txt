<h3>The challenge: who belongs in R-Ladies?</h3>

<p><a data-t="44">So here's the challenge that we faced.</a> <a data-t="47">I saw this quote on social media.</a> <a data-t="50">I was still trying to make up my mind</a> <a data-t="52">on whether to get in touch with the R-Ladies community.</a> <a data-t="55">If someone called me a lady, I would last,</a> <a data-t="58">but it seems to be inclusive.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="61">To understand why this is a problem for us,</a> <a data-t="63">you have to understand more about our community.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="70">R-Ladies is a worldwide organization</a> <a data-t="72">that promotes gender diversity in our community</a> <a data-t="75">via meetups and mentorship in a friendly and safe environment.</a> <a data-t="80">And I want to pause here and be explicit</a> <a data-t="82">about what gender diversity means to us.</a> <a data-t="85">Gender diversity is not just promoting the work</a> <a data-t="88">of a cis woman like myself and a group of cis men.</a> <a data-t="92">Gender diversity also includes individuals</a> <a data-t="94">that are trans men, trans women, non-binary, and agender,</a> <a data-t="99">and that is not an exhaustive list.</a></p>

<h3>Technical and social infrastructure</h3>

<p><a data-t="243">Managing all of this requires an enormous amount</a> <a data-t="246">of infrastructure, or I should say supporting all of this.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="251">And we think about our infrastructure in two buckets.</a> <a data-t="254">We have our technical infrastructure,</a> <a data-t="256">which are our tools, our software, and our platforms.</a> <a data-t="260">And we also have our social infrastructure,</a> <a data-t="263">which is our guidelines and resources.</a> <a data-t="266">Think about documentation</a> <a data-t="268">that tells you how to get things done</a> <a data-t="270">and who to ask for for help.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="274">Together, our technical infrastructure</a> <a data-t="276">and our social infrastructure</a> <a data-t="278">are what enable community participation</a> <a data-t="280">and development of personal connections.</a></p>

<h3>Pathways to inclusion</h3>

<p><a data-t="284">Of course, none of the infrastructure matters</a> <a data-t="287">if we don't have people.</a> <a data-t="288">So how do we get people?</a> <a data-t="291">There's a blog post from 2022</a> <a data-t="293">that outlined pathways to inclusion in a community.</a> <a data-t="297">We start off with awareness.</a> <a data-t="299">I've heard of this thing.</a> <a data-t="301">Understanding.</a> <a data-t="303">I understand what this is about.</a> <a data-t="307">Identification.</a> <a data-t="308">I can see myself doing this.</a> <a data-t="311">Access.</a> <a data-t="312">I can physically, logistically, and financially do this.</a> <a data-t="317">Belonging.</a> <a data-t="318">I feel like I fit in here.</a> <a data-t="320">And ownership.</a> <a data-t="322">I care enough to take responsibility for this.</a> <a data-t="325">I can tell you for me personally,</a> <a data-t="327">it took me about seven years to go on this pathway.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="332">We can also think about how our infrastructure</a> <a data-t="334">supports this pathway to inclusion.</a> <a data-t="337">So, for example, on the technical side,</a> <a data-t="339">we have our social media.</a> <a data-t="341">We have website.</a> <a data-t="342">We have community Slack.</a> <a data-t="343">We have Zoom.</a> <a data-t="344">We have a directory.</a> <a data-t="345">We have one password.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="348">Then we also have our guidelines and resources</a> <a data-t="350">on the social infrastructure side.</a> <a data-t="353">We have our branding, our mission statement,</a> <a data-t="356">our code of conduct, the rules.</a> <a data-t="361">And if you have your own community</a> <a data-t="361">and you're not really sure where to start,</a> <a data-t="364">I would encourage you to start here</a> <a data-t="366">with your mission statement and your code of conduct.</a> <a data-t="369">These are things that are really going to help individuals</a> <a data-t="371">understand what your community is about,</a> <a data-t="373">what you're aiming to achieve,</a> <a data-t="375">and how they're going to do that in a safe way.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="437">And I also encourage you to think about your community</a> <a data-t="440">very broadly.</a> <a data-t="441">For example, I would consider a group of open source</a> <a data-t="445">package developers a community</a> <a data-t="447">that requires social infrastructure</a> <a data-t="449">that tells people how to onboard</a> <a data-t="451">and how to participate when they want to contribute code.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="458">Beyond this, we have different people in our organization</a> <a data-t="462">that just have different needs.</a> <a data-t="465">Chapter organizers, global team, leadership team.</a> <a data-t="467">And we have different questions we need answered,</a> <a data-t="470">like how to actually get things done</a> <a data-t="472">and how to organize events.</a> <a data-t="474">So, for them, we have our organizer slack</a> <a data-t="477">and our internal guide,</a> <a data-t="478">which is also public-facing.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="482">And to be clear,</a> <a data-t="484">all of this comes back down to the global team,</a> <a data-t="486">which develop and maintain that centralized infrastructure.</a></p>

<h3>Local autonomy within a global organization</h3>

<p><a data-t="495">And even though we have this centralized infrastructure,</a> <a data-t="499">we are part of a global organization</a> <a data-t="502">and needs across the world vary.</a> <a data-t="504">So, as Nadeja Serow said,</a> <a data-t="507">I believe best practices depend on how the chapter organizers</a> <a data-t="510">can adapt to the reality to make people comfortable</a> <a data-t="515">at coming to the meetups and also being speakers.</a></p>

<h3>Addressing the rebranding challenge</h3>

<p><a data-t="643">So let's go back to that example,</a> <a data-t="644">the challenge that we face.</a> <a data-t="649">I'm still trying to make up my mind</a> <a data-t="651">on whether to get in touch with the R Ladies community.</a> <a data-t="655">If someone called me a lady, I would lash,</a> <a data-t="657">but it seems to be inclusive.</a> <a data-t="660">This is from a person who on social media is openly trans.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="667">And this is a problem for us</a> <a data-t="669">because this person has hit that first rung</a> <a data-t="673">to their pathway to inclusion.</a> <a data-t="674">They know we exist,</a> <a data-t="677">but they don't know if they belong with us.</a> <a data-t="681">And that's a problem.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="686">So we created a new process</a> <a data-t="689">and some new infrastructure in order to address this challenge.</a> <a data-t="694">In April of this year,</a> <a data-t="695">we opened an issue on our community repository</a> <a data-t="698">in our R Ladies GitHub organization.</a> <a data-t="702">The title of this issue is</a> <a data-t="703">Rebranding from R Ladies to R Ladies Past.</a> <a data-t="707">We talk about the mission of our organization</a> <a data-t="710">and how our name doesn't necessarily support our mission.</a> <a data-t="714">We've gotten feedback about this over the years.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="717">We invite people to comment on this issue.</a> <a data-t="721">Talk to us about what you think.</a> <a data-t="723">Do you think it's a good idea to stay R Ladies?</a> <a data-t="726">Should we become R Ladies Plus?</a> <a data-t="728">Should we become some name entirely different</a> <a data-t="730">that we haven't thought of?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="733">We talk about the pros of this.</a> <a data-t="735">This change could be meaningful for members of our community</a> <a data-t="739">to identify with what we do better.</a> <a data-t="741">We talk about the cons of this.</a> <a data-t="743">We have really large brand name recognition</a> <a data-t="746">and it could be hard to shift that.</a> <a data-t="749">And there's other cons as well.</a> <a data-t="751">Because remember, we are a global organization</a> <a data-t="754">and we have to respect the needs of our local communities.</a> <a data-t="758">And in some countries, being a minority gender is deemed illegal.</a> <a data-t="763">Which means that in those countries,</a> <a data-t="765">hosting an event branded as R Ladies Plus</a> <a data-t="769">could pose a risk to event organizers</a> <a data-t="771">and attendees for persecution.</a></p>

<blockquote><a data-t="758">And in some countries, being a minority gender is deemed illegal.</a> <a data-t="763">Which means that in those countries,</a> <a data-t="765">hosting an event branded as R Ladies Plus</a> <a data-t="769">could pose a risk to event organizers</a> <a data-t="771">and attendees for persecution.</a></blockquote>

<p><a data-t="756">So we created the best social infrastructure that we could.</a> <a data-t="760">There's always room for improvement.</a> <a data-t="767">But what we did is we had a very long and detailed readme</a> <a data-t="767">about how this community discussion would operate.</a> <a data-t="770">We talked about the timeline.</a> <a data-t="772">We talked about the ways you could contribute comments</a> <a data-t="776">either by commenting on the GitHub discussion forum</a> <a data-t="779">or by submitting an anonymous comment via a form.</a> <a data-t="784">We talked about the expectations in terms of</a> <a data-t="786">please contribute your discussion in English as a common language.</a> <a data-t="790">And we also talked about how we were going to have moderators</a> <a data-t="793">for this discussion to ensure that the code of conduct was followed.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="799">And then of course, we had our technical infrastructure</a> <a data-t="802">to make this happen.</a> <a data-t="803">We used GitHub as a platform for discussion.</a> <a data-t="806">We used Airtable to collect the form responses.</a> <a data-t="811">And then we used Slack as a tool to communicate about that</a> <a data-t="815">or be notified.</a> <a data-t="817">We had a really nice Airtable automation</a> <a data-t="820">so that when a new form was submitted,</a> <a data-t="821">it sent a message directly to Slack that looked like this.</a> <a data-t="825">A new anonymous comment has been submitted.</a> <a data-t="827">And that way we knew automatically to review it</a> <a data-t="831">for posting on the forum.</a></p>

<h3>What we learned</h3>

<p><a data-t="837">And I'm not here today to tell you</a> <a data-t="839">the result of the decision.</a> <a data-t="842">The rebranding decision is in progress.</a> <a data-t="844">And you're welcome to go look at all of the thoughts</a> <a data-t="846">that were contributed.</a> <a data-t="848">We are so grateful that the community came in</a> <a data-t="851">and talked about this with us</a> <a data-t="853">because we did not feel capable of a leadership team</a> <a data-t="856">of seeing all the points of view</a> <a data-t="858">in our narrow limited worldview.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="860">And we learned so much from this discussion</a> <a data-t="864">that even among cis women, people don't like the word ladies.</a> <a data-t="869">And either for personal reasons or like bigger reasons</a> <a data-t="875">that it imposes feelings and sentiments of colonization.</a> <a data-t="881">And people supplied so many different great ideas</a> <a data-t="885">in terms of why we should be stay our ladies,</a> <a data-t="888">move to our ladies plus, or be some other name entirely.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="894">And again, going back to a global organization,</a> <a data-t="897">it's really important to us that whatever name we have</a> <a data-t="902">resonates with people across the world.</a> <a data-t="905">If your native language is not English,</a> <a data-t="907">whatever name we have has to be readily understood to you.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="914">But we learned something even more important</a> <a data-t="916">through this process.</a> <a data-t="919">We learned that regardless of the outcome,</a> <a data-t="921">we need better social infrastructure</a> <a data-t="924">to facilitate the pathways to inclusion</a> <a data-t="927">for gender diverse members of our community.</a> <a data-t="930">There's a lot more that we could be doing.</a></p>

<h3>Thoughts for your community</h3>

<p><a data-t="934">So thoughts for your community.</a> <a data-t="938">Thriving communities require</a> <a data-t="940">both technical and social infrastructure.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="945">That technical infrastructure, almost the easy part,</a> <a data-t="951">it satisfies your operational needs</a> <a data-t="954">and we're here because we like doing tech things.</a> <a data-t="958">The social infrastructure, I would argue, is the hard part,</a> <a data-t="961">writing down that documentation and getting it right.</a> <a data-t="965">And the social infrastructure, I would also argue,</a> <a data-t="967">is the most important part</a> <a data-t="970">because it's going to nourish the growth</a> <a data-t="972">and longevity of that community.</a> <a data-t="974">It's going to be what sustains it,</a> <a data-t="975">where that tech stack can change over time.</a></p>

<blockquote><a data-t="958">The social infrastructure, I would argue, is the hard part,</a> <a data-t="961">writing down that documentation and getting it right.</a> <a data-t="965">And the social infrastructure, I would also argue,</a> <a data-t="967">is the most important part</a> <a data-t="970">because it's going to nourish the growth</a> <a data-t="972">and longevity of that community.</a></blockquote>

<p><a data-t="979">And in a global community</a> <a data-t="982">or in any diverse community that you have,</a> <a data-t="985">balancing that standardization and flexibility</a> <a data-t="988">is going to foster community success.</a> <a data-t="991">Thank you.</a></p>

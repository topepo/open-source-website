<h3>What the workshop covers</h3>

<p><a data-t="91">We'll be looking at things like how to build mental maps for your students to use, how</a> <a data-t="96">to give them the sort of details that they will rely on after the workshop, but there's</a> <a data-t="100">no hope of them really memorizing in completion during your one or two day workshop.</a> <a data-t="106">We'll look at learning theory, we'll look at how to design a course to keep your students</a> <a data-t="110">motivated and interested, and how to deliver your content in a way that the students will</a> <a data-t="114">remember, in a way that works with how the human brain is set up.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="119">And then finally, we'll look at how you could use some technology, such as RStudio Cloud</a> <a data-t="123">or Learner Tutorials, to actually increase how much your students will go remember after</a> <a data-t="127">the workshop.</a> <a data-t="128">This is where workshops are working against us.</a> <a data-t="131">You only have a limited amount of time with your students, but it takes time and repetition</a> <a data-t="136">and forgetting and remembering and coming back to a subject before a student can really</a> <a data-t="140">master it.</a> <a data-t="141">We can't deliver that in half a day, one day, two days, but we can use technology to follow</a> <a data-t="146">up with the students and help them build those neural connections that will go make your</a> <a data-t="149">material last and persist in their memory, and I want to show you how to do that.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="0">Hello everyone.</a> <a data-t="8">It's Wednesday night and I just sat down at my mom's bar, Sneakers.</a> <a data-t="14">I can hear the band doing their soundcheck and I take a bite of my buffalo chicken and</a> <a data-t="19">I look across the bar and see my mom sit down next to a regular.</a> <a data-t="24">She strikes up a conversation with them and introduces them to my neighbor sitting a few</a> <a data-t="29">seats down.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="31">I've witnessed this so many times at Sneakers that it's become commonplace to me.</a> <a data-t="38">But lately, probably the last year or so, I've thought a lot about the community building</a> <a data-t="44">that she's done so naturally the past 38 years.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="50">And when I say growing up in a restaurant, I really mean that.</a> <a data-t="56">This is my baby picture on the bar there at Sneakers.</a> <a data-t="61">But jumping ahead, I'd say just about 30 years from there, I am now our manager of our professional</a> <a data-t="70">community at </a><a href="/software/rstudio/">RStudio</a><a data-t="70">.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="73">In my role, I get to work with a lot of you all, either in the room or watching virtually,</a> <a data-t="80">and help connect customers to be able to share use cases and best practices and teach</a> <a data-t="87">lessons learned with one another.</a> <a data-t="91">In doing so, I've learned that a lot of what makes us really good at our job is not what's</a> <a data-t="99">apparent in our resume or through our prior job experience or through the degree that</a> <a data-t="106">we have.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="108">So when I think about my role in organizing and building community at RStudio, I realize</a> <a data-t="115">I've been running my own bar.</a> <a data-t="117">There are four, well, there's so many things that you can learn from restaurant experience</a> <a data-t="125">itself, but there are four key takeaways that I want to share with you all today that you</a> <a data-t="132">can take in building communities of your own or growing communities of your own.</a></p>

<h3>Find your Taco Tuesday: be consistent</h3>

<p><a data-t="140">So the first one, find your Taco Tuesday or be consistent.</a> <a data-t="148">At Sneakers and at a lot of restaurants, I'm sure we know, there are certain days that</a> <a data-t="155">are often associated with certain foods or certain days that are associated with certain</a> <a data-t="162">events.</a> <a data-t="164">At Sneakers, this could be having tacos on Tuesday or live music on Wednesday and Saturday.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="175">And people don't come to every single event, but when they have a free night or when they're</a> <a data-t="182">looking for something to do with friends, they know exactly where they can go.</a> <a data-t="188">And so I try to do this with our events at RStudio as well.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="194">So every Tuesday at 12 Eastern time, I host the RStudio Enterprise Community Meetup.</a> <a data-t="202">And this is a meetup event for people to just share what they're doing.</a> <a data-t="207">So they may share a new use case or a best practice learned and present for 30 minutes</a> <a data-t="215">and take questions from the community.</a> <a data-t="219">And so these happen every Tuesday at 12 Eastern time.</a> <a data-t="223">And it may be people gathering around an industry group like using R in healthcare or more broadly</a> <a data-t="230">using </a><a href="/software/shiny-r/">Shiny</a><a data-t="230"> in production.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="235">Also every Thursday at 12 Eastern time, I host our Data Science Hangout, which I see</a> <a data-t="242">a few people here who have been to those as well.</a> <a data-t="246">This is a little bit less formal.</a> <a data-t="249">Not that the other is formal at all either, but there's no presentation at these.</a> <a data-t="255">It's just a different data science leader from the community who joins me to answer</a> <a data-t="259">questions from the community.</a> <a data-t="262">So all the questions are audience-led from you all and could be questions like, how do</a> <a data-t="268">I talk to executives?</a> <a data-t="271">Or how are you thinking about hiring right now?</a> <a data-t="275">Should I have a centralized or decentralized data science team?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="280">These are every Thursday at 12 Eastern time.</a> <a data-t="284">I've included the link to the calendar for these if you ever want to add the individual</a> <a data-t="289">events or just the whole series of events to your calendar.</a> <a data-t="295">This one's best formatted for your phone, but if you're on your desktop, add an extra</a> <a data-t="300">dash and type calendar, and it will look a lot nicer too.</a> <a data-t="304">But if you're also an organizer, I just wanted to include that the tool that I use here is</a> <a data-t="309">called Add Event.</a> <a data-t="312">I really like that.</a> <a data-t="313">It makes it really easy to share events that people can just put on their calendar without</a> <a data-t="318">having to give me any information.</a> <a data-t="320">So find your Taco Tuesday.</a></p>

<h3>Start conversations across the bar</h3>

<p><a data-t="323">Secondly, start conversations across the bar.</a> <a data-t="327">So as I opened up with that scene, imagine the regular sitting alone at the bar.</a> <a data-t="335">My mom might walk over to them and strike up a conversation with them and the couple</a> <a data-t="340">sitting a few seats down.</a> <a data-t="343">It might be about the band that's playing next week that she knows that they both like,</a> <a data-t="350">or it could be about a special that's on the menu that somebody else ordered across the</a> <a data-t="354">bar too.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="356">The topic itself doesn't really matter as much.</a> <a data-t="360">It's just that she's giving permission for that interaction to happen and kicking off</a> <a data-t="365">the conversation.</a> <a data-t="368">She might start the conversation and then have to go walk away and run to the kitchen</a> <a data-t="372">and help with something or make a drink behind the bar.</a> <a data-t="376">But that person sitting alone at the bar might stay a little bit longer because they now</a> <a data-t="381">know somebody.</a> <a data-t="383">Or they might come back more regularly because they feel a part of the sneakers community.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="391">I think one of the sneakers regulars said it best, and this is a newspaper article from</a> <a data-t="397">a few years ago.</a> <a data-t="399">But Deb is someone who I've known for many years now.</a> <a data-t="403">She's filmed employee weddings.</a> <a data-t="405">She actually made a video for my high school volleyball team.</a> <a data-t="410">But Deb said, it's the kind of place where you can go in alone, have some food and a</a> <a data-t="414">glass of wine, and pretty soon you are a part of the conversation.</a></p>

<blockquote><a data-t="410">But Deb said, it's the kind of place where you can go in alone, have some food and a</a> <a data-t="414">glass of wine, and pretty soon you are a part of the conversation.</a></blockquote>

<p><a data-t="420">I want people to feel this way at our studio community events as well, minus the food and</a> <a data-t="427">wine except for this week.</a> <a data-t="431">But as community organizers, we have a responsibility to bring people together based on shared interests.</a> <a data-t="439">And I think it's important here that these interests aren't something that we ourselves</a> <a data-t="443">have to be experts on.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="447">But we can go a step further here to help kick off conversations with people at events</a> <a data-t="454">as well.</a> <a data-t="456">I think one of my favorite examples of this comes from the Data Science Hangout, which</a> <a data-t="462">happens every Thursday at 12 Eastern time.</a> <a data-t="467">But this particular week featured Kristy Angel, a data science manager from Stitch Fix.</a> <a data-t="474">And leading up to that session, a few people had reached out to me on LinkedIn or maybe</a> <a data-t="481">at meetups as well and mentioned that they're really having a hard time finding a new job.</a> <a data-t="490">And they were hitting roadblocks with recruiters and not getting through to a hiring manager</a> <a data-t="495">because they didn't have a certain degree.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="499">So I had the privilege of knowing this as the organizer of the group and getting to</a> <a data-t="504">talk with all these people.</a> <a data-t="506">So I was able to ask this to Kristy for them anonymously, just to get her insights and</a> <a data-t="512">hear her experience.</a> <a data-t="516">And I started to see in the chat that everybody started commenting with their own degrees</a> <a data-t="525">and their own non-data science degrees in a room full of data scientists, or a Zoom</a> <a data-t="531">room full of data scientists.</a> <a data-t="534">And it was just one of those moments that you just remember, just seeing people all</a> <a data-t="542">supporting each other and cheering each other on for their non-data science degrees and</a> <a data-t="549">supporting people who are running into this right now.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="554">So start those conversations across the bar.</a></p>

<h3>Touch every table</h3>

<p><a data-t="558">Third, touch every table.</a> <a data-t="563">So at Sneakers, it's pretty much a guarantee if you go in for dinner, my mom will come</a> <a data-t="569">by your table and thank you for coming in.</a> <a data-t="573">If it's her night to work, of course.</a> <a data-t="574">If it's her business partner Annie's night to work, she'll be the one to come by.</a> <a data-t="580">And it doesn't matter if you are regular or this is your first time coming into Sneakers.</a> <a data-t="587">She'll stop by and say thank you for coming in and ask where you're from, how you're enjoying</a> <a data-t="594">your meal, or maybe reminiscing with customers who have become like family.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="600">I actually had no idea this was a term until preparing for this talk, but table touching</a> <a data-t="607">is a real thing in the restaurant industry.</a> <a data-t="610">And there's a lot of articles about how touching every table can help create a guest for life.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="618">In the virtual world, I want to be able to stop by and meet you at your table as well.</a> <a data-t="628">The way that I like to do this is when people join a meetup or a data science hangout, is</a> <a data-t="636">to write down the names of people who mentioned they're joining for the very first time, or</a> <a data-t="645">maybe it's just a name that I haven't seen joined before.</a> <a data-t="648">But also write down a few names of people who have been coming back regularly, or faces</a> <a data-t="655">that I've been seeing on Zoom.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="660">This is not possible, I know, with hundreds of people, but I don't think that's a reason</a> <a data-t="664">to not do it at all.</a> <a data-t="666">So I might say to myself, today I'm going to write down the names of five regulars.</a> <a data-t="674">And five new people.</a> <a data-t="677">And I'll reach out to them on LinkedIn just to say thank you.</a> <a data-t="681">Thank you for joining, or thank you for the comment that you put into the chat.</a> <a data-t="687">So it might be just a few people that week, but hopefully, as we go, I get to reach out</a> <a data-t="693">to everybody.</a> <a data-t="695">So touch every table.</a></p>

<h3>Notice the unsaid</h3>

<p><a data-t="699">And the last takeaway I want to leave you with, which also expands on the last one.</a> <a data-t="707">Sometimes when you're going by and visiting with a table, you get a chance to learn more</a> <a data-t="712">about someone's experience and how you can make that a little bit better.</a> <a data-t="718">But not only in what they're telling you, but in observing the unsaid.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="726">So I think I've given you enough restaurant examples right now that I can put you in the</a> <a data-t="731">role of owner.</a> <a data-t="734">So let's say over here we have our table of four who are waiting for trivia night to start.</a> <a data-t="743">They all ordered a round of drinks, three of them are finishing up, and you walk by</a> <a data-t="748">their table to check in on them.</a> <a data-t="751">You notice, though, that one of the drinks is pushed over to the side.</a> <a data-t="759">Now you ask them how their night's going, they say everything's great.</a> <a data-t="762">But you can tell they probably didn't enjoy their drink as much as they would have liked</a> <a data-t="768">to by the way that it's pushed over to the side.</a> <a data-t="772">So you look at that and take it as an opportunity to ask them if there's something else that</a> <a data-t="776">you could get them and insist that you want them to have something that they'd enjoy.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="784">This doesn't have to just be about untouched food or drinks.</a> <a data-t="788">It could also be about stopping by a table and noticing that it's somebody's birthday</a> <a data-t="793">and noticing that before they actually told you.</a> <a data-t="798">Just noticing the little things.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="802">This dining experience looks a little bit different in a virtual world.</a> <a data-t="808">So let me just take you on a journey here.</a> <a data-t="810">So we'll look at my, this little stick figure here, and we can imagine it as anyone, but</a> <a data-t="817">we'll call him Bob.</a> <a data-t="819">So Bob joins a meetup event for the first time and listens in.</a> <a data-t="826">Maybe Bob was on the list of one of the five people that I wrote down as a new person or</a> <a data-t="830">maybe he wasn't.</a> <a data-t="832">He comes back again and joins in listen-only mode again.</a> <a data-t="838">A week later, he comments in the chat, and then I notice Bob unmutes briefly, it pops</a> <a data-t="845">up to the top of my Zoom, but doesn't end up speaking.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="851">So I'm not going to say to Bob on LinkedIn, hey, you joined three times and you never</a> <a data-t="858">talk, what can I do to make you feel more comfortable?</a> <a data-t="863">But I might say something like, hey, Bob, thanks so much for joining today.</a> <a data-t="868">I loved your comment about learning R as a SaaS user.</a> <a data-t="873">I think that perspective was incredibly helpful.</a> <a data-t="877">If you ever have any suggestions or things that we could be doing to make this event</a> <a data-t="882">more useful, please let me know.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="887">And Bob might say, thanks for joining, I had a great time.</a> <a data-t="891">I wanted to ask a question, but I wasn't sure if it was the right format.</a> <a data-t="898">And so I've had the opportunity now to learn something more about Bob's experience by thanking</a> <a data-t="904">him for coming and by just noticing that there was something there in the unsaid.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="914">And so what Bob does for me here is I have that opportunity to say, did you know that you can ask questions anonymously as well?</a> <a data-t="931">And so I can let him know this is a place for any question to be asked, but there's</a> <a data-t="940">something more there.</a> <a data-t="940">And he points out to me that I didn't make it clear maybe in the beginning of the presentation</a> <a data-t="946">that people could ask questions anonymously.</a> <a data-t="950">And if I said that once, maybe that's not enough.</a> <a data-t="954">Because if somebody joined a few minutes later, they would have missed that.</a> <a data-t="959">So it lets me know that that's something I should put in the chat as well.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="965">So months later, maybe he keeps coming back and keeps joining, and eventually presents</a> <a data-t="972">at the meetup as well.</a> <a data-t="974">I think this is one of my favorite things about community building, is seeing somebody</a> <a data-t="980">come for the very first time, and then starting to get to know people, and you see them, like,</a> <a data-t="988">being friends on Twitter or commenting on each other's LinkedIn posts.</a> <a data-t="992">And then eventually that person being the one presenting at the event too.</a></p>

<blockquote><a data-t="974">I think this is one of my favorite things about community building, is seeing somebody</a> <a data-t="980">come for the very first time, and then starting to get to know people, and you see them, like,</a> <a data-t="988">being friends on Twitter or commenting on each other's LinkedIn posts.</a> <a data-t="992">And then eventually that person being the one presenting at the event too.</a></blockquote>

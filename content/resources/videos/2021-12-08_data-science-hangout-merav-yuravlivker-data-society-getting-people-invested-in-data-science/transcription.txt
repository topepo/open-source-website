<h3>Measuring success of data science projects</h3>

<p><a data-t="835">There was an anonymous question earlier that was, how do you measure the success of a data science project or client engagement? But also, how do you help companies define this?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="858">Yeah. The key to defining success is defining the metrics that you want to look at, and that's true of a data science project.</a> <a data-t="866">It's true of any of the programs that we run, because at the end of the day, you could say, is a policy that we've developed successful, but if you don't know what metrics to measure, it's very hard to answer that question.</a> <a data-t="877">So the first thing that we want to do, that we do, and that I would recommend for anyone when starting out with a data science project is, what's the key question you're trying to answer, and what are the metrics that you're going to use to answer that question?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="886">So for example, if you want to take a look at the effectiveness of a marketing campaign, for example, the question could be maybe how many people want to buy a certain product or clicked on a particular ad, and then what are the metrics that you're using?</a> <a data-t="906">Well, you're probably using people who click on it, but maybe you also want to take a bit click-through rate.</a> <a data-t="912">Maybe you'll see that out of a thousand people that saw it, only five people clicked on the ad.</a> <a data-t="918">Is that a successful rate for you?</a> <a data-t="919">You would have to compare that against other baseline rates that you've done.</a> <a data-t="924">If you've run another campaign that maybe had a hundred clicks, then maybe that's not a successful campaign.</a> <a data-t="930">But if this campaign is garnering clicks and another one didn't, then I would consider that a success as well.</a> <a data-t="935">So you want to take the baseline measures, you want to make sure you're defining your metrics correctly, and you want to also make sure you're answering, asking the right question.</a> <a data-t="952">A lot of times, asking the right question and then getting the right data, there's a bit of a back and forth there, because if you don't ask the right question at first, you might be collecting data and metrics that are not relevant.</a> <a data-t="969">So what you want to do is just make sure that the metrics are well-defined before starting any of those projects, and that will help define success.</a></p>

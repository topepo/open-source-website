<h3>DevOps, blameless postmortems, and data analysis</h3>

<p><a data-t="1203">So, like, throughout, so we kind of have this, like, timeline of what this looks like, like, evolving over time.</a> <a data-t="1210">And one of the things, and, again, this was, like, four and a half years ago.</a> <a data-t="1212">I was still working at Etsy.</a> <a data-t="1214">And Etsy had this kind of amazing DevOps team.</a> <a data-t="1217">And so, for those of you who don't know, DevOps stands for developer operator.</a> <a data-t="1222">And it's this field where, essentially, it was, like, kind of taking IT to the next level.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1228">So it was saying the people who run the website, the people who keep the servers on for the website, the people who do everything to kind of keep this, you know, shopping website up, they should also be the people developing the tools to keep the website up.</a> <a data-t="1251">And so it's, like, developer operator.</a> <a data-t="1254">And at Google, they kind of have a different word for it, site reliability engineer, but it's the same thing.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1261">And I was really inspired by that work and kind of how they approach problems.</a> <a data-t="1265">One of the central tenets was this idea of a blameless postmortem, where if you ran into a problem, like, let's say your system failed and the website went down, there is a human tendency to, like, blame the person who wrote the code that failed or whatever.</a> <a data-t="1293">But Etsy focused a lot on, instead, doing this thing called a blameless postmortem, where you talk about, like, you frame the problem as though the system failed the operator rather than the operator failing within a good system.</a> <a data-t="1321">And then that opens up the conversation to, like, talk about iterating on the system and saying, you know, okay, this person went to work this day, said they wanted to do a good job.</a> <a data-t="1336">They did not say they wanted to take down the website.</a> <a data-t="1340">But then the system, like, allowed them to take down the website.</a></p>

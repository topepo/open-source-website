<p><a data-t="23">I teach data science to mostly undergraduates and it can be an intimidating subject to them.</a> <a data-t="29">I often get asked for rules of thumb that they can use to make good data science decisions.</a> <a data-t="35">And while there are some rules of thumb that work in a lot of situations, I want my students</a> <a data-t="40">to come away with a deep understanding of the concepts so that when they're in a situation</a> <a data-t="46">where that rule of thumb doesn't apply, they're not just stuck.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="50">I decided that guided simulation exercises were a great way to create that deeper understanding.</a> <a data-t="57">By my definition, guided simulation exercises are activities where you're asked to simulate</a> <a data-t="62">data, you apply an algorithm or a computation to that data, and then you're given specific</a> <a data-t="69">guided questions that can help you figure out what that simulation can tell you about</a> <a data-t="74">whichever algorithm or computation you applied to the data.</a></p>

<h3>Why simulation exercises work</h3>

<p><a data-t="79">Simulation exercises are helpful for three main reasons.</a> <a data-t="82">First, I think it encourages this attitude of exploration when learning.</a> <a data-t="88">And second, it gives you tools to test your intuition or try weird edge cases that you</a> <a data-t="93">think of and see what happens.</a> <a data-t="96">And finally, I think it empowers this deep understanding of the concepts that you're learning.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="103">A lot of people learn data science because they think they should, but my goal is to</a> <a data-t="108">help people really deeply explore the concepts that they're learning.</a> <a data-t="113">And sure, they could write their own simulations, but I think the guided part is really important.</a> <a data-t="119">I think these exercises are a little more practical than just throwing people in the</a> <a data-t="124">deep end on their own.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="126">It makes simulating data in R feel more approachable, and it gives people tools to try and figure</a> <a data-t="133">things out themselves rather than just relying on what they're told.</a> <a data-t="137">Relatedly, the guided nature of these exercises gives people the tools to learn and try things</a> <a data-t="144">on their own and tailor their experience to things that specifically make them curious.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="149">For example, maybe you're wondering, what would happen if I ran principal component</a> <a data-t="153">analysis on a set of variables that are completely uncorrelated?</a> <a data-t="158">Well, try it out.</a> <a data-t="160">Or maybe you're wondering why we would ever use k-fold cross-validation rather than the</a> <a data-t="166">simpler, more computationally efficient train-test split.</a> <a data-t="170">Well, run a bunch of simulations and see what the difference is.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="174">In my opinion, the best data scientists are the ones who have a little bit of doubt about</a> <a data-t="179">whether the established method is the best way, necessarily.</a> <a data-t="184">And these guided simulation exercises encourage people to test things out, to push on the</a> <a data-t="191">boundaries of established methods, and to figure out how our favorite algorithms work</a> <a data-t="196">in atypical situations.</a> <a data-t="199">After all, our data will not always be well-behaved.</a></p>

<blockquote><a data-t="174">In my opinion, the best data scientists are the ones who have a little bit of doubt about</a> <a data-t="179">whether the established method is the best way, necessarily.</a></blockquote>

<p><a data-t="203">Guided simulation exercises are not a replacement for the math and theory that go behind data</a> <a data-t="209">science topics, but it can help support them.</a> <a data-t="212">And I think the examples, the specific questions, and the established code help make simulation</a> <a data-t="219">more accessible, especially to people who don't have the skills yet or maybe don't have</a> <a data-t="224">the time to set all of it up on their own.</a></p>

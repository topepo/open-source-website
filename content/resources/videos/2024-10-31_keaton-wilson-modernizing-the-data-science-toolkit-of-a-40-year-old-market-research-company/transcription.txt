<h3>Why modernize the toolkit</h3>

<p><a data-t="322">So back to turning the ship.</a> <a data-t="324">I came on at KSR about a year and a half ago, and I think one of the things we talked</a> <a data-t="331">about when we were defining my job responsibilities and what the plan was for the next year was</a> <a data-t="337">thinking about the areas that we wanted to turn the ship in, but also why we're trying</a> <a data-t="342">to modernize the toolkit at KSR, and I think those whys fall into three main buckets.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="348">The first is that statistical rigor doesn't always equal optimized code, right?</a> <a data-t="353">KSR has a foundation of really building strong math behind our data analyses over the 40</a> <a data-t="359">years that the company's been in existence, but things have changed, right?</a> <a data-t="363">And we can now optimize code for a lot of different things, so for speed, for understandability</a> <a data-t="368">and modularity, and so that was one of the big sort of reasons why we want to try and</a> <a data-t="374">upgrade things.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="376">The second is that we offer data products that are software.</a> <a data-t="380">A lot of that are the shiny apps that we develop, but also automated reports as well, and we</a> <a data-t="386">can apply some basic but modern software development practices to improve the consistency and the</a> <a data-t="393">functionality of those products.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="397">And finally, the evolution of the field.</a> <a data-t="399">Things have just changed, right?</a> <a data-t="400">Things have changed a lot in 40 years, period.</a> <a data-t="403">Things have changed a lot in data science over the last decade and the languages that</a> <a data-t="407">we work in.</a> <a data-t="408">There are a lot of new tools to bring to bear that can help us generate better work.</a></p>

<h3>Clear navigation</h3>

<p><a data-t="458">For each of these guiding principles, I want to provide a short case study that sort of</a> <a data-t="463">dives in a little bit on how we applied them.</a> <a data-t="466">The first is our migration to GitHub.</a> <a data-t="468">So this happened right when I came on board, or shortly thereafter, and we decided this</a> <a data-t="474">was a high-priority thing to try and work on.</a></p>

<h3>Managing expectations</h3>

<p><a data-t="558">On to managing expectations.</a> <a data-t="562">So for us, this is composed of four pieces.</a> <a data-t="565">Thinking about leadership's expectations versus your expectations, I think often those two</a> <a data-t="570">things are different, and finding alignment is really important, or was really important</a> <a data-t="574">for us.</a> <a data-t="576">The second is thinking about on-the-job learning.</a> <a data-t="579">It's something that takes time and often needs to be balanced with the business needs of</a> <a data-t="583">the organization.</a></p>

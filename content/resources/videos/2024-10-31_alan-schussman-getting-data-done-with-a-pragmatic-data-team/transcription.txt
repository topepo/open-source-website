<h3>Setting expectations honestly</h3>

<p><a data-t="405">And I have a few ways that over a few years, I think I've had some success.</a> <a data-t="413">The first one, and this applies both to folks who are hiring, to team leaders, and also to folks who are, to data scientists, folks who are looking for jobs.</a> <a data-t="424">Both sides, all of these folks involved need to set expectations really carefully and really honestly so that we're describing the work that we have in our teams in an accurate way.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="438">So here's some bullet points from job descriptions for roles on my teams.</a> <a data-t="444">And you'll see that there's a progression here.</a> <a data-t="444">Develop and build data assets and models.</a> <a data-t="451">Okay, in the service of like business insights for our stakeholders.</a> <a data-t="454">This is the sort of like data science top line where people go, hey, that sounds good.</a> <a data-t="457">Building data sets, gathering value from data, presenting it to stakeholders.</a> <a data-t="462">This sounds cool.</a> <a data-t="465">Become a subject matter expert for needed processes and data domains.</a> <a data-t="471">Okay, this still sounds like it's in the realm of things that I want to practice, although maybe it's getting a little close into the like sort of application side of things or the business process side of things.</a> <a data-t="481">And that's not necessarily what some folks are signing up for.</a> <a data-t="486">And then there's the end where we have to solve problems in this space all the time.</a> <a data-t="492">Build and support capability, work on integrations, work on pipelines, work on getting data from place to place so that we can partner with analysts and with others to do good work with it.</a></p>

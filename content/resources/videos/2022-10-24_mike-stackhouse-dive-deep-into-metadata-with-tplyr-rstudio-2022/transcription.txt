<h3>About the Tplyr package</h3>

<p><a data-t="185">So just to give a little bit of background on the package itself, Tplyr, it's an R package that we released back in 2020, and I shamelessly took the name from </a><a href="/software/dplyr/">dplyr</a><a data-t="185"> and the principle of dplyr that it's a package, a grammar of clinical summary tables.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="223">So the reason that we try to make it a grammar of summary tables is that we wanted to take your focus off of doing the actual summary and thinking about the algorithms that you need to write to do the summarization and how you get the numbers that you want and really design it more based off of describing the output that you want to create.</a> <a data-t="243">So shifting that focus of just saying, this is what I want to build.</a> <a data-t="249">A statistician, typically you get your CSR and you get the mocks of the tables that you need to produce, and that's going to have different characteristics that are going to need to be fulfilled.</a> <a data-t="258">So we wanted Tplyr syntax to focus on describing the output and building up the characteristics and then saying go and build this for me.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="269">The last thing was that we also wanted to really draw our line in the sand.</a> <a data-t="274">I did not want to anchor into any specific reporting package itself.</a> <a data-t="278">I favored Huxtable early on because it had good RTF support.</a> <a href="/software/gt/">GT</a><a data-t="283"> has been building up over the last few years and it's had an amazing place.</a> <a data-t="286">Jessica loves FlexTable.</a> <a data-t="290">And then in the Shiny world, you have things like Reactable.</a> <a data-t="294">So I wanted to make the things that come out of our package really easily accessible to any styling package that you want to create.</a> <a data-t="300">And we just wanted to really stop at the character formatting and the numbers so that that annoying part was done for you and that you can pop it into some other library that will actually make it pretty.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="296">To describe Tplyr itself, I want to thank Christina Fillmore for giving me the best metaphor for this that I've had, which is think about cake.</a> <a data-t="301">So cake can take a lot of forms.</a> <a data-t="301">It can have a lot of flavors and it can have a lot of different decorations.</a> <a data-t="305">You can have multiple layers into your cake and those things can look differently depending on how you build it up.</a> <a data-t="314">And that's really what a Tplyr table ends up being.</a></p>

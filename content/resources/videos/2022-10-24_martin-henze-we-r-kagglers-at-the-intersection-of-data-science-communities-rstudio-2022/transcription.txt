<h3>Hidden gems initiative</h3>

<p><a data-t="600">In fact, two years ago, I decided to start an initiative around the Kaggle notebooks.</a> <a data-t="623">What I'm talking about is an initiative to discover and highlight great Kaggle notebooks, which, for whatever reason, haven't gotten the attention and kudos from the community that they deserve.</a> <a data-t="636">I'm calling them hidden gems.</a> <a data-t="636">And after reading hundreds of notebooks and writing quite a few myself, I would like to share with you what Kaggle taught me on how to write a great notebook, right?</a> <a data-t="649">So here are a few tips that will be illustrated by different hidden gems.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="657">So first of all, you want to introduce your work to capture your reader's attention and draw them into your analysis.</a> <a data-t="663">A couple of topical images or illustrations go always well with a few opening paragraphs that explain the goals, the context, and set the scene for what's to follow.</a> <a data-t="671">So like Laura here, you could use an image of cocoa beans to lead into your flavorful analysis of chocolate taste ratings.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="682">Explaining your methodology will help your reader to follow your analysis through its various steps.</a> <a data-t="687">And this can be part of your introduction.</a> <a data-t="687">And you can even go as far as Ram Shankar did and build a flow chart out of little pictograms that explain the different steps of your analysis.</a> <a data-t="697">And this is really some next level methodology communication right there.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="707">Great visuals, of course.</a> <a data-t="707">They are at the heart of many fantastic notebooks.</a> <a data-t="714">And whether it's for exploration or for communication, you want your data visualizations to be clear and accessible in communicating the insights that you derived.</a> <a data-t="721">And that can mean simple visuals, but it can also mean complex but well designed.</a> <a data-t="727">So in this example here, Daniel was creating a visual for climate models and rising CO2 levels that would not look out of place in a science or nature paper on some high-profile website.</a> <a data-t="736">It's meticulously designed with insets and annotations and definitely takes a little time to digest.</a> <a data-t="742">So like with all of them, if you want to learn more, definitely check out the notebook.</a></p>

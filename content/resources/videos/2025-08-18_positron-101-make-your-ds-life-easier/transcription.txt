<p><a data-t="0">This is how to use </a><a href="/software/positron/">Positron</a><a data-t="0">'s layout to make your data science life easier.</a> <a data-t="3">When you're working with data, keeping track of your variables, plots, and outputs can</a> <a data-t="6">get messy fast.</a> <a data-t="7">Positron was built with that in mind.</a> <a data-t="9">Here's how its layout helps you stay organized.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="11">First when you open up Positron, you'll notice there's three main areas.</a> <a data-t="14">There's the script pane.</a> <a data-t="15">This is where you'll write your code.</a> <a data-t="16">There's the console pane, where you'll access the terminal and see results and errors.</a> <a data-t="20">And then there's the side panes.</a> <a data-t="21">This is where things get really useful for your data workflows.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="23">This will give you a live view of your data frames and variables, their types and shapes,</a> <a data-t="27">and quick summary you can click into.</a> <a data-t="28">You can literally click on a data frame variable and it will open up a spreadsheet-style viewer.</a></p>

<h3>The plots pane</h3>

<p><a data-t="32">Then there's a pane for visualizations.</a> <a data-t="34">When you run a plot command with matplotlib, seaborn, or plotly, the chart will pop up</a> <a data-t="37">in the plots tab of the output pane.</a> <a data-t="39">You can scroll, zoom, and even export it with one click.</a></p>

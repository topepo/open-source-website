<h3>CSS, theming, and customization</h3>

<p><a data-t="2013">But there are a few questions in the chat and the Slido on customization, CSS, being able to look at the gallery examples and, you know, make your website, your blog look the way that you'd like.</a> <a data-t="2025">But any kind of tips or feature, upcoming features on making like that kind of stylization easier?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2034">So one that I will point to for 1.3 that we did and is available in the 1.3 build right now, it's not super well documented.</a> <a data-t="2042">But I think if you find the release notes for 1.3, there's a link to some better documentation.</a> <a data-t="2045">One of the one of the one great thing about Quarto is the grid system that it has that sort of lays out the page and lets you lay out different elements and have sidebars and things.</a> <a data-t="2056">And that's a great thing about it.</a> <a data-t="2057">That's also a terrible thing about it because it's quite opinionated about the size of everything.</a> <a data-t="2061">And so that's been a challenge for people, I think, who have different opinions about the size of things, not they don't share our opinions.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2070">So starting in 1.3, we've tried to make it a lot easier for people to actually control the grid system and call the size and position, not necessarily the position, but basically to say, you know, how what do you want your widths and margins to be inside of our grid system?</a> <a data-t="2085">And that lets you make the content wider or make the sidebar smaller or make the sidebar wider.</a> <a data-t="2090">And we've got pretty simple ways for you to configure those things, be either YAML or SCSS that should cascade through the grid system appropriately.</a> <a data-t="2102">So if people have a chance to give that a spin, you know, I found it makes a really big difference for things like sidebars that have long text or other scenarios where you really get sort of you get some pain from the fact that Quarto has such strong opinions about how narrow and wide things need to be.</a> <a data-t="2118">So that's a simple, simple thing for people to play with starting in 1.3.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2123">Aside from that, I think the one other thing that Carlos just did that is fun is extending our theming to Mermaid, which is really nice for using Mermaid diagrams.</a> <a data-t="2134">You won't if you're in a dark theme, you won't be confronted with a glaring, bright diagram, but instead a nice, consistent themed diagram.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2143">So one of the things that we are working on for 1.3, so the Mermaid theming was one of those things that Charles mentioned, is actually sort of documenting and figuring out what are the CSS and SCSS variables that are available in general for a theme so other packages can use them as well.</a> <a data-t="2163">So we forward SCSS variables to proper CSS variables.</a> <a data-t="2166">So if you're just producing on your own custom JavaScript or your own custom Python output things like we know we're making, we're going through the trouble of figuring out how to make those available for you.</a> <a data-t="2176">So they're themed automatically and things of that sort.</a> <a href="/people/rich-iannone/">Rich Iannone</a><a data-t="2181">, who's the author of GT, who is the package for sort of producing really nice tables, has been working with me on that.</a> <a data-t="2189">So we're looking forward to have the kind of like really nicely integrated support for theming that will work consistently sort of across packages so that folks that are authoring these can just actually have a consistent look depending, independent of like the theme they chose or the environment in which things are executing.</a></p>

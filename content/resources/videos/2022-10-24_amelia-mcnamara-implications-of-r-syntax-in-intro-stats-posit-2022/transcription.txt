<h3>The head-to-head comparison</h3>

<p><a data-t="79">So I did a semester-long head-to-head comparison</a> <a data-t="79">of the formula and tidyverse syntaxes. Why did I do this to myself? I basically doubled</a> <a data-t="85">my work, right? I had to create the materials in both syntaxes. I tried to make them as</a> <a data-t="90">consistent as possible in R markdown documents that had the same text. The only thing that</a> <a data-t="94">was different was the R syntax. <h3>Results</h3>

<p><a data-t="111">So here are my results. Some things are really</a> <a data-t="111">easy regardless of syntax. <p><a data-t="121">But different things can be hard. So in the formula syntax, dealing</a> <a data-t="126">with and explaining how we deal with missing data, that's pretty challenging. There's options,</a> <a data-t="131">there's na.rm equals true, there's use equal complete ofs. That was tough. In the tidyverse,</a> <a data-t="137">dealing with and explaining the relationship between two categorical variables was a challenge.</a> <a data-t="141">So we're used to seeing two categorical variables explained in a two-way table. That's not a</a> <a data-t="146">tidy data structure. And it's harder for me and for students to conceptualize of the relationship</a> <a data-t="151">when it's in that tidy format. <h3>How many functions do students really need?</h3>

<p><a data-t="160">The other thing that I was really interested</a> <a data-t="160">in is how much R code you really needed in an intro stat course. And it turns out not</a> <a data-t="165">that much. So in the formula section, I exposed students to 37 functions. In the tidyverse</a> <a data-t="170">section, they saw 50 functions. Those are a little bit different in terms of numbers.</a> <a data-t="175">But both of them over the course of 15 weeks, totally reasonable. A big criticism of the</a> <a data-t="179">tidyverse in teaching is, oh, there's so many tidyverse functions. How would students ever</a> <a data-t="183">learn them all? Well, they're not going to learn them. You're just going to show them</a> <a data-t="186">the 50 that they need.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="186">And if you look at the top five in each of these sections, I</a> <a data-t="190">think you'll kind of see some of the differences here as well.

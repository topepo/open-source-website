<h3>Introducing the Spatial Equity Data Tool</h3>

<p><a data-t="149">So kind of why at a high level, did we create the spatial equity data tool?</a> <a data-t="154">So we've noticed in the past few years that there's been an increased demand across governments, across nonprofits and other organizations to reliably assess and measure spatial equity.</a> <a data-t="164">And so to respond to that, my team developed the spatial equity tool and then I have since built on it for the last two years.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="172">So at a high level, what does the tool do?</a> <a data-t="176">So this is kind of the workflow we think about here.</a> <a data-t="179">So you can upload to the tool any point vector spatial data.</a> <a data-t="184">So what does that mean?</a> <a data-t="184">It means anything that you can plot as a series of individual points on a map.</a> <a data-t="189">That's the type of data the tool takes.</a> <a data-t="192">So you upload it to the tool, the tool does a lot of work and then it returns to two sets of scores.</a> <a data-t="197">It returns these demographic disparity scores and geographic disparity scores.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="203">So what are those scores?</a> <a data-t="205">So this is an example screenshot from our spatial equity data tool for demographic disparity scores.</a> <a data-t="210">For the next two slides, we have examples from this New Orleans 311 call request data from 2014 to 2018.</a> <a data-t="220">311 is a service that many cities provide it.</a> <a data-t="222">So as you may know, it sounds kind of like 911, but you would call 911 for an emergency.</a> <a data-t="228">311 is a service you could call for kind of non-emergencies.</a> <a data-t="231">So say like your streetlight has gone out or you have a pothole that you need filled.</a> <a data-t="234">311 may be the way you could reach out to your city to to get that service.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="241">OK, so that's what 311 is.</a> <a data-t="241">What is this demographic disparity score?</a> <a data-t="243">So what the demographic disparity score does is it looks across the geography as a whole and it makes a comparison.</a> <a data-t="250">So we say, hey, looking at, in this case, the census tracks where these points or where these 311 call requests come from, how do the demographics of those areas compare to the city as a whole?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="317">OK, so that's the first set of scores.</a> <a data-t="317">The second set of scores are a geographic disparity score.</a> <a data-t="320">So this looks at something different, whereas the demographic disparity score says, hey, let's look across the geography as a whole.</a> <a data-t="326">The geographic disparity score says, I want to look at a specific subgeography.</a> <a data-t="331">So for the city scale, the subgeography, again, is a census tract.</a> <a data-t="334">And we do kind of simple arithmetic, a simple difference in proportions here.</a> <a data-t="339">So we look at the proportion of the data points in a census tract that we upload and the proportion of a population.</a> <a data-t="345">So if the proportion of data points is greater than the proportion of the population, that area receives a positive disparity score and it's colored in blue.</a> <a data-t="354">And we might think of that as that area is being overrepresented by those points.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="359">Conversely, if there are, if the proportion of data is less than the proportion of the population, we would say that that area gets a negative disparity score.</a> <a data-t="369">So it's kind of the yellow and orange areas.</a> <a data-t="371">And we might think that that area is underrepresented by the data.</a></p>

<h3>What is Monte Carlo simulation?</h3>

<p><a data-t="331">Okay, so Monte Carlo simulation, what is it?</a> <a data-t="335">For those of you who maybe have a little bit more background in it, this is not a textbook definition, but I think it's really useful for conveying the idea.</a> <a data-t="341">It's an analytical method that evaluates a lot of potential combinations of uncertain inputs, drawing from the range of possibilities of each of those inputs, and calculates the overall outcome.</a> <a data-t="352">So if you could think of a function k, or a value k being the function of three input variables, x, y, and z.</a> <a data-t="358">If x, y, and z are not single point values, but rather are themselves distributions, Monte Carlo simulation helps you answer the question, well, what is the distribution of k?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="370">And so, you know, and what are the real world impacts of different segments of k's distribution?</a> <a data-t="377">So if each of these variables yield a k, an output variable k that's normally distributed, the middle of that distribution is going to have one potential outcome, but the high and low tails are going to have very different impacts upon your real world operation.</a> <a data-t="393">And so Monte Carlo simulation helps you understand what, both what is the outcome distribution, and what are the impacts of those.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="400">This is really commonly used in finance for, you know, stress testing is a, that the Federal Reserve requires is an application of it.</a> <a data-t="406">Oil and gas industry uses this because they need to understand what is the potential financial impact of, say, drilling a new well, or any sort of investment.</a> <a data-t="417">And in my field, supply chain, Monte Carlo simulation can be used in a number of different ways.</a> <a data-t="423">One, it can be used to understand the impact of uncertain sales in the future upon different warehouses and trucking segments in the supply chain.</a> <a data-t="434">It can also be used to understand investment decisions around, hey, do I need to buy more trucks?

<h3>ICT and education during COVID-19</h3>

<p><a data-t="103">Let us look at one of the aspects covered in the survey, ICT.</a> <a data-t="107">We are going to look at the influence that ICT has had on education in Kenya during the</a> <a data-t="112">COVID pandemic.</a> <a data-t="113">But first, a few statistics.</a> <a data-t="116">According to a new report by UNICEF, two out of three school-age children do not have internet</a> <a data-t="122">access at home.</a> <a data-t="123">In fact, the number of unconnected pupils is higher in Africa and Asia as compared to</a> <a data-t="129">the rest of the world.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="131">The first COVID case was announced in Kenya on 12 March and two days after, the government</a> <a data-t="138">announced closure of schools and institutions were encouraged to move their learning online.</a> <a data-t="143">The Ministry of Education also made a directive for the curriculum teaching to be delivered</a> <a data-t="147">via radio, TV, YouTube and other digital platforms.</a> <a data-t="151">And so, online learning began.</a> <a data-t="154">Kenyans adapted very quickly.</a> <a data-t="156">One university trained their students and staff and also entered into a partnership</a> <a data-t="160">with a mobile network operator to ensure that their staff received cheaper data bundles</a> <a data-t="165">specifically for e-learning.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="168">Several other institutions did their best to heed to the government's directive.</a> <a data-t="174">Entertainment programs were aired on TV during school hours and young kids quickly learned</a> <a data-t="179">how to use tools such as Zoom and Google Hangouts.</a> <a data-t="184">But then, online learning was not going well for everyone.</a> <a data-t="187">Leaders from semi-arid areas came out claiming that online learning in their areas was just</a> <a data-t="192">but a fallacy because their areas lacked basic infrastructure.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="197">So, what basic infrastructure is required for smooth online learning?</a> <a data-t="202">First, one will need a laptop or a mobile phone to access materials in electronic format.</a> <a data-t="208">And these devices have to actually be connected to the internet so that you can actually access</a> <a data-t="213">this information and electricity is needed to power both.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="218">A lot of students took to social media to complain that online learning was not working</a> <a data-t="222">for them.</a> <a data-t="223">A lot claimed that they lacked the minimum basic requirements needed to partake in online</a> <a data-t="228">learning.</a> <a data-t="228">Electricity has also been a problem in Kenya.</a> <a data-t="232">Even for us who have access to electricity, it's not reliable.</a> <a data-t="236">I'm actually hoping that I'll be done with this presentation by the time lights go off.</a> <a data-t="240">You can visit the Kenya Power Twitter handle to just see more of the complaints that we</a> <a data-t="248">channel through that platform complaining about blackouts day in, day out.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="280">Charlie, on the other hand, comes from a humble background.</a> <a data-t="284">His parents are not able to pay school fees for him and so he has to depend on the area's</a> <a data-t="289">bursary fund.</a> <a data-t="290">And after emerging top in the Kenya Secondary National Examination, Charlie got a scholarship</a> <a data-t="296">to also attend Shule Kubwa.</a> <a data-t="299">So Charlie and Manzi, together with some of the friends they grew up with, are in the</a> <a data-t="304">same class.</a> <a data-t="305">And while in school, they are subjected to the same conditions, the same lecture room,</a> <a data-t="309">the same lecture period, the same exam, and so on.</a></p>

<h3>What is gganimate?</h3>

<p><a data-t="50">What is gganimate?</a> <a data-t="50">gganimate is a package</a> <a data-t="50">I have wrote.</a> <a data-t="50">It's kind of a two-fold thing.</a> <a data-t="58">It's both a grammar of graphics, like a</a> <a data-t="58">theoretical backbone of how to describe animation, and it's also an implementation of said</a> <a data-t="65">theoretical backbone.</a> <a data-t="70">In the same sense that there exists a grammar of graphics theory,</a> <a data-t="70">and </a><a href="/software/ggplot2/">ggplot2</a><a data-t="70"> is just one of many different implementations of this theory, what I wanted</a> <a data-t="75">to do with gganimate was also to kind of build a theoretical backbone for how to address</a> <a data-t="81">animation and data visualization.</a> <a data-t="88">It's two-fold, and on top of that, it's an extension to ggplot2,</a> <a data-t="88">so it's not something that exists on its own.</a> <a data-t="94">It is something that you can apply to any of</a> <a data-t="94">your normal ggplot2 plots and make them move magically.</a></p>

<h3>What is Shiny?</h3>

<p><a data-t="372">Yeah.</a> <a data-t="372">So to me, Shiny is fundamentally about letting data scientists create interactive web applications to take their analysis that they're already doing on their own machine and taking the insights that they are gaining from their data on their own machine and being able to expose that to usually other people, sometimes to themselves, but usually to expose that to other people in a really dynamic and interactive way.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="406">In the past before Shiny, I think you would see, I mean, it's still very common to issue a static report and send it to someone.</a> <a data-t="413">And if there were lots of variables in your analysis, if there were lots of different ways you might want to slice and dice the data, you would either just add more and more pages to that PDF or HTML page.</a> <a data-t="438">And we had a lot of people tell us that they would exchange PDFs with thousands of pages and good luck trying to find the page that you want.</a> <a data-t="451">Or you would do this back and forth of like, okay, here's my best guess at what you want.</a> <a data-t="455">And then you'd have this endless email back and forth of, how about this? <p><a data-t="519">Thank you.</a> <a data-t="519">And I see an anonymous question that came in a little bit ago, which was, what's the elevator pitch? And then maybe further discussion points for investing in Shiny rather than using point-and-click BI tools.</a></p>

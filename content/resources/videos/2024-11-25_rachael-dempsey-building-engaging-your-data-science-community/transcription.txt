<p><a data-t="202">So first, start small.</a> <a data-t="207">I think you can have a tendency to think</a> <a data-t="209">that a larger audience means a better event.</a> <a data-t="213">And I don't think that's true.</a> <a data-t="214">Starting small is really important</a> <a data-t="216">for building the culture that you want for your group.</a> <a data-t="219">It helps you in getting people to know each other</a> <a data-t="224">but also that core group of people</a> <a data-t="226">will help you maintain the culture as your event grows</a> <a data-t="230">or as your community grows.</a> <a data-t="231">I think with the hangouts,</a> <a data-t="232">we started with 20 people in the beginning</a> <a data-t="236">and this is also really important for being intentional</a> <a data-t="240">about growing a diverse group from the beginning</a> <a data-t="242">and allows you to adapt based on feedback you receive.</a></p>

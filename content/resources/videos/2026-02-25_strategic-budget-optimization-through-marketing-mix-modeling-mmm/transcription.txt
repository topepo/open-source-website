<h3>What is Marketing Mix Modeling?</h3>

<p><a data-t="33">Let's first quickly introduce Marketing Mix Modeling, or MMM.</a> <a data-t="38">Rachel Dempsey and Mark Rieke shared a great metaphor for Marketing Mix Modeling, unbaking</a> <a data-t="43">a cake.</a> <a data-t="45">In marketing, we're often handed the cake, or the total revenue, and asked to figure</a> <a data-t="50">out exactly which ingredients made it taste so good, and which ones were unnecessary.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="56">MMM does this by unbaking the cake.</a> <a data-t="60">It identifies the impact of every ingredient—the flour, the sugar, or even the luck of the</a> <a data-t="66">oven temperature—to answer these sorts of questions.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="72">More concretely, MMM is an analytical approach that uses statistical techniques on marketing</a> <a data-t="80">time series data to estimate the impact of different marketing tactics on key performance</a> <a data-t="87">metrics.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="89">Executing an MMM produces a sophisticated blend of technical metrics and strategic insights.</a> <a data-t="97">On the technical side, it generates statistical outputs such as model coefficients, confidence</a> <a data-t="104">intervals, and goodness-of-fit measures.</a> <a data-t="107">On the strategic side, the model yields visual and actionable assets, including contribution</a> <a data-t="114">waterfall charts, cross-channel return-on-ad-spend comparisons, and diminishing-return curves.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="122">By reviewing these outputs, stakeholders can solve critical business uncertainties, such</a> <a data-t="129">as attribution, how many sales did a specific channel drive over the past year, predictive</a> <a data-t="136">forecasting, such as how would total sales shift if I invested X amount of dollars into</a> <a data-t="142">a specific channel next year, and optimization—what is the ideal budget allocation to maximize</a> <a data-t="149">future revenue?</a> <a data-t="151">Ultimately, these insights allow businesses to optimize their marketing investments and</a> <a data-t="157">turn historical data into a roadmap for future growth.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="164">In an MMM, inputs are all marketing variables and factors, including media, like social</a> <a data-t="171">or search, baseline factors, like seasonality or brand equity, and external factors, like</a> <a data-t="178">the economy or even the weather.</a> <a data-t="181">Outputs consist of business metrics and KPIs, such as sales revenue, market share, and customer</a> <a data-t="188">acquisition.</a> <a data-t="188">It's important to note that MMM is meant to be a holistic view.</a> <a data-t="193">It's supposed to measure outcomes for all marketing channels together in one analysis.</a> <a data-t="199">It uses aggregate data, not individual user information, and so is often considered a</a> <a data-t="205">privacy-first approach.</a></p>

<h3>Choosing your MMM tools</h3>

<p><a data-t="209">Just as there are dozens of brands of eggs, flour, and milk at the grocery store, there</a> <a data-t="214">are many ways to bake an MMM.</a> <a data-t="217">No two data science teams use the exact same ingredient.</a> <a data-t="221">Within open source, there are many options.</a> <a data-t="224">For Python, there are tools like Google's Meridian or PyMC Marketing.</a> <a data-t="229">For R, there are tools like Meta's Robin or building custom high-flexibility workflows</a> <a data-t="235">using </a><a href="/software/tidymodels/">tidymodels</a><a data-t="235"> and RStandarm, as Ryan Tsingpei detailed in his Posit 2023 talk.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="242">You also have to choose your style of baking.</a> <a data-t="245">Are you using Bayesian methods to incorporate priors, which are marketing insights that</a> <a data-t="251">can be included in the model, or a more traditional frequentist approach?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="257">But the best recipes in the world don't matter if your kitchen is a mess and your</a> <a data-t="261">oven isn't calibrated.</a> <a data-t="264">And this is where we propose Posit tools for the process.</a> <a data-t="268">While the MMM workflow shares many steps with traditional data science, the stakes are high</a> <a data-t="273">since you're justifying budget and resources.</a> <a data-t="277">Posit tools can help you develop reproducible, transparent, and defensible models for your</a> <a data-t="281">team with a code-first integrated approach.</a> <a data-t="284">And with that, I'll pass it to Dan to show you this workflow starting in </a><a href="/software/positron/">Positron</a><a data-t="284">, the</a> <a data-t="289">new data science IDE from Posit.</a></p>

<h3>The Racial Equity Dashboard</h3>

<p><a data-t="69">Our Racial Equity Dashboard is built in </a><a href="/software/shiny-r/">Shiny</a><a data-t="69"> and ggplot.</a> <a data-t="74">The framework houses tabs as separate modules for each category of data stories.</a> <a data-t="80">The landing page contains a brief introduction to the dashboard itself, our email address,</a> <a data-t="86">and a tab navigation bar for the data story categories across the top.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="95">Each of these tabs follows the same format, a brief introduction to the category, followed</a> <a data-t="101">by two to five visualizations and stories.</a></p>

<h3>Maternal mortality story</h3>

<p><a data-t="111">Let's walk through one of those stories now.</a> <a data-t="114">The definition box tells us that pregnancy-related mortality ratio, which is pregnancy-related</a> <a data-t="120">deaths per 100,000 live births, is often used as an indicator of a nation's health.</a> <a data-t="126">The expandable explanation box gives the viewer additional context about how to interpret</a> <a data-t="132">the visualization.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="135">This plot shows the 30-year trend of the pregnancy-related mortality ratio of the U.S. compared to select</a> <a data-t="141">economic peers.</a> <a data-t="143">Though these five nations began the 90s at the same level, the U.S. has since deviated</a> <a data-t="148">from the pack, nearly tripling its maternal mortality.</a> <a data-t="153">The country is currently 56th in the world.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="163">We often hear of pregnancy-related complications due to age.</a> <a data-t="167">For white mothers, this risk becomes apparent around 35 years old.</a> <a data-t="173">In black mothers, the increase starts much earlier.</a> <a data-t="177">The resulting gap means black women aged 40 or older have a pregnancy-related mortality</a> <a data-t="183">ratio almost four times that of their white peers.</a></p>

<blockquote><a data-t="177">The resulting gap means black women aged 40 or older have a pregnancy-related mortality</a> <a data-t="183">ratio almost four times that of their white peers.</a></blockquote>

<p><a data-t="189">Educational attainment is another indicator of disparity.</a> <a data-t="192">A college education appears to correlate with a lower pregnancy-related mortality ratio</a> <a data-t="196">for a white woman, but has little to no impact for a black woman.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="209">Looking specifically at North Carolina, we were able to identify a roadmap of interventions</a> <a data-t="214">based on high-risk pregnancy factors and have shared these with our community partners.</a> <a data-t="219">For example, increased access to prenatal care and pregnancy testing would have a significant</a> <a data-t="224">impact on pregnancy-related mortality ratio.</a></p>

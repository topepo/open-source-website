<h3>Challenges with organizational feedback surveys</h3>

<p><a data-t="89">Even once we've switched to digital surveys, a lot of these same feelings still persist,</a> <a data-t="96">it still affects how we think about things.</a> <a data-t="96">So what I want to talk about today is some of the</a> <a data-t="102">challenges in getting good information from employees or other people in an organization.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="162">The second point of challenge is privacy or trust.</a> <a data-t="162">Who's going to be receiving</a> <a data-t="162">the feedback and are they going to receive it in a positive light?</a> <a data-t="167">What's kind of the potential</a> <a data-t="167">impact and what's the potential harm?</a> <a data-t="172">This is important, especially when you're asking people</a> <a data-t="172">about their job.</a> <a data-t="172">No one in a feedback survey is really going to say, oh, I'm doing a poor job,</a> <a data-t="178">or they may be very hesitant to say something like, oh, I think this is going wrong, when negative</a> <a data-t="183">feedback about another department, about a project could affect their co-workers and could affect</a> <a data-t="190">their friends in a negative light.</a> <a data-t="190">So you have to really think about how can I make people feel safe</a> <a data-t="196">and feel validated to give honest responses?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="203">Finally, you have to think about impact and the</a> <a data-t="203">way it can affect things.</a> <a data-t="207">When you're providing information, you don't necessarily know how it's</a> <a data-t="207">going to be used.</a> <a data-t="214">If I say something that even if I think it's a positive light, together with other</a> <a data-t="214">information, other context, other motives from decision makers, it could have an unexpected</a> <a data-t="219">effect.</a> <a data-t="225">So when I'm filling out feedback, I may be hesitant or I may be thinking about like what can</a> <a data-t="225">go wrong? Do I trust this process?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="231">All of these pain points make collecting data hard.</a> <a data-t="231">How do I</a> <a data-t="231">get an honest, high quality view of what's going on in an organization?</a> <a data-t="238">And sometimes organizations</a> <a data-t="238">can become really desperate when they're dealing with this.</a> <a data-t="242">Either they're sending a million emails</a> <a data-t="242">and reminders constantly bugging people, or in the case of one organization I worked with, they</a> <a data-t="248">started to force people's responses where it would literally lock employees out of the system if they</a> <a data-t="253">hadn't completed the feedback surveys on time and they would have to go through and do it.</a> <a data-t="258">And that's</a> <a data-t="258">a good way to get 100% response rate, but it's not necessarily a good way to really know what's</a> <a data-t="263">going on in your organization.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="269">Is there a more organic way we can do this?</a> <a data-t="269">And that's why today</a> <a data-t="269">I'm going to be thinking through the impacts of these bad incentives on surveys.</a> <a data-t="275">They can hurt</a> <a data-t="275">data quality, they can make it hard to take these responses and actually change organizations,</a> <a data-t="280">and what can we do about it?</a> <a data-t="287">How can we get a better experience?</a> <a data-t="287">And can we use some of these</a> <a data-t="287">tools like Shiny, </a><a href="/software/quarto/">Quarto</a><a data-t="287">, or even Markdown to start to change the way we design surveys and</a> <a data-t="294">deliver results to address these pain points?</a> <a data-t="299">So that's our goal for today, to kind of think through</a> <a data-t="299">the process.</a></p>

<h3>Research context: organizational network surveys</h3>

<p><a data-t="304">So to talk through what I did, I want to give you a little bit more context about my own</a> <a data-t="304">work.</a> <a data-t="310">I'm an academic, I'm at Grinnell, and I was working on this project with several amazing</a> <a data-t="310">collaborators at Northwestern.</a> <a data-t="316">And we study networks, how people are connected to others</a> <a data-t="316">within a team or an organization.</a> <a data-t="321">And we were looking at this in several companies in the</a> <a data-t="321">context of COVID, trying to understand what is going on, how are people seeking advice, providing</a> <a data-t="326">leadership and connecting in these challenging times.</a> <a data-t="332">And the way we do this is by asking</a> <a data-t="332">questions that build these sort of network models of who do people go to for advice, where are these</a> <a data-t="338">silos in the department, where the whole network is bigger than the sum of its parts.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="344">So this is</a> <a data-t="344">really reliant on surveys.</a> <a data-t="350">You have to ask people about connections like advice, leadership, trust.</a> <a data-t="350">These are all things that only exist in people's heads.</a> <a data-t="354">They're not something that you can quantify</a> <a data-t="354">with some like digital trace data.</a> <a data-t="360">So as we started to conduct research, we were working</a> <a data-t="360">with different companies, and we would work with the management and leadership in the company.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="366">So what you typically do in this network survey environment is you survey all the employees in</a> <a data-t="370">the department, you combine all the responses, so all the employees are the individual nodes in that</a> <a data-t="376">network, and you look at the big picture, and you try to give the management some type of top-level</a> <a data-t="381">insight, takeaways, and rely on them to make changes.</a> <a data-t="388">But this is all dependent on getting</a> <a data-t="388">good data.</a> <a data-t="394">We only act on what we can see, and if you ask people a question like who's making tasks</a> <a data-t="394">difficult to complete, who do you enjoy working with, what are the challenges on your team,</a> <a data-t="400">you may not get accurate answers when you're trying to ask employees that when it's coming</a> <a data-t="404">from their boss.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="411">And that's the problem is this framing, it's focused on me as the academic or</a> <a data-t="411">the management or leadership of an organization of taking action.</a> <a data-t="416">We gather data, we put the data</a> <a data-t="416">together, we get a good report, and we see what's going on.</a> <a data-t="423">That kind of framing though isn't putting</a> <a data-t="423">the participants that are actually taking time to fill out the survey first.</a> <a data-t="427">We're not thinking</a> <a data-t="427">about their own user experiences, and that's kind of the big challenge.</a> <a data-t="434">So how do we rethink this?</a></p>

<h3>Rethinking the approach: bottom-up change</h3>

<p><a data-t="434">In the model before, if we wanted to improve the organization, fix problems, it's all about sort of</a> <a data-t="441">top-down change.</a> <a data-t="441">We have this network map, we give some key insights to management, and management or</a> <a data-t="447">leadership is going to change something, and they're going to fix something about the organization.</a> <a data-t="451">They're going to have one cohesive strategy.</a> <a data-t="451">We started to ask what's the alternative?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="458">What if instead of trying to just help management, we tried to give value directly to the participants</a> <a data-t="463">in the survey?</a> <a data-t="463">So instead of the management knowing everything, people being concerned about</a> <a data-t="468">their privacy and trust, will they use the information well?</a> <a data-t="474">What if we give every individual</a> <a data-t="474">their own tiny piece of the puzzle?</a> <a data-t="479">We give them information about their own local network based</a> <a data-t="479">off of the responses they provide, and we empower all of them to make changes.</a> <a data-t="484">Changes are going to</a> <a data-t="484">come from the bottom up, and hopefully help out everyone in the company.</a></p>

<blockquote><a data-t="474">What if we give every individual</a> <a data-t="474">their own tiny piece of the puzzle?</a> <a data-t="479">We give them information about their own local network based</a> <a data-t="479">off of the responses they provide, and we empower all of them to make changes.</a> <a data-t="484">Changes are going to</a> <a data-t="484">come from the bottom up, and hopefully help out everyone in the company.</a></blockquote>

<p><a data-t="490">So that's what we started</a> <a data-t="490">to do with some of these companies.</a> <a data-t="496">So we started building this tool called the Personalized Network</a> <a data-t="496">Report, and the big picture idea that we had for this is, can we take your survey responses, and can</a> <a data-t="503">we give you back value based off of them?</a> <a data-t="509">So instead of taking your survey responses, fill this out,</a> <a data-t="509">you'll never directly hear from us again after it's done, and we're going to give some aggregate</a> <a data-t="513">information to your boss that you're going to hope does something well with it, and hope your</a> <a data-t="517">responses aren't harmful to others.</a> <a data-t="523">Can we take your responses, and can we deliver value to you?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="524">So in some sense, we're just circling back the data that they provide to them, but in some other</a> <a data-t="531">senses, we're giving them context on how to interpret it, insights on how to look at a network,</a> <a data-t="536">what does it mean, how do I compare to others on average, and helping them think through the process.</a> <a data-t="541">And that's the idea, to help every individual make changes, rather than trying to just benefit</a> <a data-t="548">management and just give information to management.</a> <a data-t="548">Completely rethinking power balance.</a></p>

<h3>The personalized network report in practice</h3>

<p><a data-t="555">So what did this look like?</a> <a data-t="555">When we were running it with organizations, it was moving from something</a> <a data-t="561">that felt like running a survey, to something more like a professional development workshop.</a> <a data-t="566">We'd have participants fill out this questionnaire, they would describe their networks, they would get</a> <a data-t="570">this personalized feedback report, and then we would go into the companies and we would do a</a> <a data-t="575">workshop with them.</a> <a data-t="575">We'd talk them through how to interpret the report, how to use this information,</a> <a data-t="580">how to think about their networks, and make strategies for how to leverage them.</a> <a data-t="584">So it was</a> <a data-t="584">all data driven, but we were running it more like a professional development workshop, focusing on</a> <a data-t="589">helping everyone, rather than just running the survey that's just some task on a big list they</a> <a data-t="596">needed to complete.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="602">In practice, for the implementation, the questionnaire itself,</a> <a data-t="602">we were just running something using Qualtrics.</a> <a data-t="607">We would then take the information from the</a> <a data-t="607">Qualtrics API, put it into PDF reports in Markdown, and then distribute it to the employees via email.</a> <a data-t="613">So very basic stuff, there's nothing technically really sophisticated here,</a> <a data-t="618">but the big picture was thinking through what does this process look like.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="623">So in building this questionnaire, we were kind of walking people through this process of how do I</a> <a data-t="627">map out my network.</a> <a data-t="627">I'm picking a context in the workplace, I'm identifying key people,</a> <a data-t="632">I'm describing network connections between them, and thinking about everyone and how everything's</a> <a data-t="637">interrelated.</a> <a data-t="637">And what we saw is that even with this very simple, very straightforward-seeming</a> <a data-t="643">approach, we were able to get good results.</a> <a data-t="649">We felt that we were able, participants said they</a> <a data-t="649">were able to better make changes and better understand their network, and overall, without</a> <a data-t="655">giving any information to the management or having to do like any direct exchange, we were able to</a> <a data-t="660">get better quality data for our own research.</a> <a data-t="666">So we were protecting participants' privacy, and we</a> <a data-t="666">were doing interventions in the organization from this bottom-up perspective rather than a top-down</a> <a data-t="672">one.</a></p>

<h3>Why it worked: incentives and reflection</h3>

<p><a data-t="678">So why did this work?</a> <a data-t="678">And these are some of the most important points I want to make in my</a> <a data-t="678">presentation today.</a> <a data-t="686">The reason participants found value and the reason they provided good information</a> <a data-t="686">is because we were meeting participants and trying to help them.</a> <a data-t="690">They were filling out something</a> <a data-t="690">that was for their own benefit and where we were engaging them with the results.</a> <a data-t="695">So the incentives</a> <a data-t="695">were right.</a> <a data-t="695">When we sent out these reports, we were giving them tools to better understand the data.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="703">So our reports looked something like this.</a> <a data-t="711">The very first version was just very simple PDFs,</a> <a data-t="711">but we were helping people map out their networks.</a> <a data-t="716">We were describing different properties of their</a> <a data-t="716">networks and giving them their own score compared to other different groups in their organization</a> <a data-t="722">and other groups in general, helping them understand what do I want my network to look like?</a> <a data-t="727">How can I strategize about it?</a> <a data-t="727">What do I want to change?</a> <a data-t="733">And by taking this sort of expertise that</a> <a data-t="733">we had and turning it into something accessible to the survey respondents, we were able to empower</a> <a data-t="740">them to understand, reflect, and make changes.</a></p>

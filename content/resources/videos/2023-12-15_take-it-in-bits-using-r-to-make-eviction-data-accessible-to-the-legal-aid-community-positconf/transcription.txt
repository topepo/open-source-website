<h3>About Legal Services Corporation and the Civil Court Data Initiative</h3>

<h3>Outcomes and impact</h3>

<p><a data-t="1039">And so it took us from this data pipeline to a slightly revised version where we still had the data source and the data lake, but rather than going from standardized data sets directly to the end user, essentially what we were doing was just going by way of, you know, data memos for RMarkdown and passing that along.</a> <a data-t="1057">Again, this diagram is a little bit oversimplistic in a way, because I think that it implies that we stopped sending data or spreadsheets, which isn't true.</a> <a data-t="1070">We had data memos, but we really used those as just sort of a jumping off point, as a way to get the ball rolling, to get the conversation going around sharing data.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1084">And what ended up happening is, like I said, folks came back to us and asked us, you know, can I have this data?</a> <a data-t="1089">And then the spreadsheets that we were sending them were things that were actually important, things that they actually wanted, which is obviously always key when you're sending off data.</a> <a data-t="1101">We found that, you know, we went from sending off data that nobody had asked for and nobody was looking at, to folks actually, like, not being able to send data off fast enough.</a> <a data-t="1112">We had to grow our team because we couldn't respond to all of the specific data requests that we were getting, which is always a great thing to do.</a></p>

<blockquote><a data-t="1101">We went from sending off data that nobody had asked for and nobody was looking at, to folks actually, like, not being able to send data off fast enough.</a></blockquote>

<p><a data-t="1124">And so some of the outcomes from this work, there were a couple of different things.</a> <a data-t="1129">Principally, we had improved data accessibility and understanding.</a> <a data-t="1134">So, again, folks were no longer having to, you know, work in Excel to find an answer to their specific question.</a> <a data-t="1141">They could just have that analysis done for them.</a> <a data-t="1151">This also improved access via just sharing the information directly.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1155">Beyond that, we had time efficiency.</a> <a data-t="1157">And this is both time efficiency on our end, because we weren't coming up with custom data sets to send off in anticipation of a specific legal aid organization's needs.</a> <a data-t="1166">Instead, we were zooming out to the state level and automatically generating these reports.</a> <a data-t="1172">You know, we put them on, for those of you familiar with Amazon Web Services, we put them on an EC2 instance in Amazon Web Services and kind of just let it run in the cloud.</a> <a data-t="1185">And it does that every day, or excuse me, every week.</a> <a data-t="1189">So, every Monday, I log on to my computer and just sort of double check the output of the Word documents and make sure that, you know, everything looks good.</a> <a data-t="1201">We also have more efficient tests that are running programmatically.</a> <a data-t="1207">But as a final check, that's really what I do every Monday is just over my cup of coffee, I look at each individual document and I make sure that it's looking good.</a> <a data-t="1216">And that's the extent of it.</a> <a data-t="1218">And then we can just sort of let them live there and share them and, you know, revisit things on a quarterly basis, rather than having to regenerate new documents every week or for every individual occasion.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1243">On the part of the legal aid organizations, again, not having to do the analysis with our data, that's a huge time saver.</a> <a data-t="1253">And then the last thing is just increased conversation, which is really what our end goal is, is to be able to talk to folks and, you know, get their insight of how the data can best help them.</a> <a data-t="1265">And that's definitely a downstream of this.</a></p>

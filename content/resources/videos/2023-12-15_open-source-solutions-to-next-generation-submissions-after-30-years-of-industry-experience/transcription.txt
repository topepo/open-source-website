<h3>The Pharmaverse and industry collaboration</h3>

<p><a data-t="378">So what does this change look like?</a> <a data-t="380">Well, there's a collection of R packages called the Pharmaverse that helps the industry with all aspects of preparing for this regulatory submission.</a> <a data-t="389">So data manipulation, tables, listings, and figures, and presentation of those tables, listings, and figures, as well as things like infrastructure bits or environment management, also validating R packages and testing R packages.</a> <a data-t="406">So there's a huge amount of work done in here.</a> <a data-t="410">The other great thing is this is work done across a huge number of different companies.</a> <a data-t="414">And as you can see here, a significant number of people.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="419">So this is all great information that you can tap into and exploit and see how much of it is useful to you for your process today.</a> <a data-t="427">But it's not just R packages that people are developing.</a> <a data-t="430">People are developing training modules.</a> <a data-t="432">So this is a great example where Genentech have made their training course on open source development and using GitHub to develop new things and how to work in that framework, and then made it available to any of us in this room to go and evaluate this course and see how it works.</a> <a data-t="451">And that's fantastic, because it brings all of our skill levels up.</a> <a data-t="455">So when we ask, how do we do this, we can all now work off that same level playing field of knowledge.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="464">A lot of these initiatives are powered by consortium.</a> <a data-t="467">So there are three listed here, FUSE, R Consortium, and Open Source Informer.</a> <a data-t="473">And what these consortia do is that they give a platform for multiple different companies to come together and discuss freely.</a> <a data-t="481">But not just companies.</a> <a data-t="482">It's also the regulatory agencies.</a> <a data-t="485">In this R Submissions working group from R Consortium, the FDA are active members and engaged, and they are defining how they want things to be, because they want to shape what that next generation reporting looks like.</a> <a data-t="504">And I think that's really powerful.</a> <a data-t="506">Rather than it being powered by pharma companies who want to do something fancy, the regulators themselves are sitting at the table and going, well, we have a stake in this.</a> <a data-t="515">We want to tell you, we want to shape what you're going to do with this.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="519">But these three organizations are driving that change in a way that, you know, I've never seen anything like this in the last three years, last 30 years.</a> <a data-t="528">I would say that in the last five years, the pace has picked up enormously.</a> <a data-t="533">Even in the last two years, it's accelerated again.</a> <a data-t="536">So these consortia really are powering that change.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="542">You might say to yourself, why would companies share this knowledge?</a> <a data-t="547">Isn't that our intellectual property?</a> <a data-t="549">And 30 years ago, yes, if I wanted to talk to someone in Roche about how Pfizer do things, our internal legal people would get rather twitchy.</a> <a data-t="559">But if we do it through consortia now in a working group, that gives us this level playing field that says anyone can come and talk at this within this working group.</a> <a data-t="567">And it frees us to be open and candid about how we're doing things and try to find the best way.</a> <a data-t="572">Because it's not just Pfizer want to find the best way.</a> <a data-t="576">We want to find the best way and hear what Roche are doing and Genentech are doing and all these other companies, and then help that inform how we move forward.</a></p>

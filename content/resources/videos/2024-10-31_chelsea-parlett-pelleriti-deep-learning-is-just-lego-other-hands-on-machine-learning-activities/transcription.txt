<p><a data-t="466">And this is one of those concepts where when someone explains it to you like that, you might go, oh, okay, I get it.</a> <a data-t="477">You know how sometimes when a teacher is doing something in front of you, either coding or on a whiteboard, and you go, I totally get that.</a> <a data-t="482">And then when you're forced to actually do it on your own, you go, hmm, maybe I didn't get that quite as much as I thought I did.</a> <a data-t="495">Well, this coloring sheet is the perfect example of an activity that forces you to confront your knowledge so that you can actually tell if you understand a concept.</a></p>

<h3>Designing hands-on activities</h3>

<p><a data-t="591">Now, you might not be teaching a deep learning class or you might not even be a teacher at all.</a> <a data-t="596">But I want to talk to you a little bit about how I think about creating these activities so that whether you're in the boardroom or the classroom, you'll have an idea of what kind of activities might work for you.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="587">The first thing that I think about is I want to design activities that remove the mental load.</a> <a data-t="600">Like our Lego convolutions, we want to do something physical that allows people to have an easier time processing or thinking about the information we're presenting.</a> <a data-t="606">If it's difficult for me to imagine the matrix algebra that's happening inside of a computer vision model, then maybe I can offload some of that to a physical object like a Lego convolution.</a> <a data-t="618">This allows easier accessibility to that knowledge and also just makes it more intuitive, even for those that do have an easy time of visualizing it.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="631">Second, I want to design activities that leverage physical or visual intuition that people have about how the world works.</a> <a data-t="637">If I can build an activity that allows people to take a concept that might be a little bit abstract and put it physically in the world, that means they might get to use the information they have about how they expect the world to work in order to understand that concept.</a> <a data-t="654">Just like our clay gradients.</a> <a data-t="655">If I see this 3D object in front of me, I can sort of sense how would a ball roll down this hill, which allows me to understand the math better.</a></p>

<h3>The story of family separation</h3>

<p><a data-t="42">But first we have to start from the beginning</a> <a data-t="42">with the broader story of family separation.</a> <a data-t="48">So in November of 2017, a Congolese woman</a> <a data-t="48">and her six-year-old daughter arrived at a legal port of entry in California to seek</a> <a data-t="52">asylum.</a> <a data-t="59">They were detained together at first, but after four days in detention, ICE put</a> <a data-t="59">the child on a plane and flew her 2,000 miles away from her mother to a facility in Chicago.</a> <a data-t="65">Before she was sent to Chicago, S, her daughter, was close enough for Ms. L to hear her screaming</a> <a data-t="69">through the night.</a> <a data-t="73">They only got to talk to each other around six times for the four months</a> <a data-t="73">that they were separated.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="79">In February of 2018, ACLU lawyers met with</a> <a data-t="79">Ms. L and immediately they filed a class-action lawsuit, because already by February, it</a> <a data-t="84">was clear that this was not an isolated case.</a> <a data-t="92">In March of 2018, Ms. L and her daughter were</a> <a data-t="92">reunited largely in response to pressure from this litigation.</a> <a data-t="98">But in that same month, then-DHS</a> <a data-t="98">Secretary John Kelly proposed on CNN that family separation might be a useful policy</a> <a data-t="103">to help deter immigration from Central America.</a> <a data-t="112">In May, this policy became official and was</a> <a data-t="112">met with immediate backlash, both from the legal community but also from activists, from</a> <a data-t="118">regular people, from families.</a> <a data-t="123">This was a huge story that I'm sure you saw over the</a> <a data-t="123">summer and it really enraged everyone, as it should have.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="128">In response to that, in June, on June 20th, Trump finally administered an executive order</a> <a data-t="134">to stop the separation, but this executive order didn't have anything to do with the</a> <a data-t="138">more than 2,000 children who were already separated.</a> <a data-t="142">And so the judge in the Ms. L case</a> <a data-t="142">issued an injunction and ordered the government to reunite the families.</a> <a data-t="147">And those efforts</a> <a data-t="147">are still continuing today.</a> <a data-t="153">Most of the families have either been reunified or their wishes</a> <a data-t="153">have been sort of gathered and understood.</a> <a data-t="158">But this has been a really, really long and</a> <a data-t="158">arduous process.</a> <a data-t="164">And it's been clear the whole time that there was not a data system in place</a> <a data-t="164">to handle the reunifications.</a></p>

<h3>The data challenge</h3>

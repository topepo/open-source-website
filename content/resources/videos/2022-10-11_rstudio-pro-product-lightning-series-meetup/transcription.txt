<p><a data-t="223">So for those who aren't aware what Package Manager is, it's one of our tools for centralizing the governance of your data science packages.</a> <a data-t="236">So really what you can do, everything from mirroring your public repositories internally.</a> <a data-t="244">So whether you are looking at using a CRAN or bioconductor or PyPI public repository,</a> <a data-t="252">you want to bring that inside your network and host it from a server locally in your network.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="300">And then most importantly, for today anyway, what we're going to talk about is how you can share internally developed packages using Package Manager.</a> <a data-t="310">So there are, the concept of revolves around what we call local package sources, which allow you to take a,</a> <a data-t="318">build your packages, however you normally would do that, normally using like, you know, R directly to build or any of the tools in there, and then add those to your local source in Package Manager.</a></p>

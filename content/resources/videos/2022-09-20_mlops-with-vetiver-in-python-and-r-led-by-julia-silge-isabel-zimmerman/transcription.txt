<h3>What MLOps means: version, deploy, monitor</h3>

<p><a data-t="380">So, MLOps is versioning.</a> <a data-t="382">This is managing change in models.</a> <a data-t="385">It's going to help you avoid the pitfalls of, you know, model final, and then model final one, and then, like, model final, it's actually this one.</a> <a data-t="395">So, it helps you scale your model, but it also will help you organize your models and share your models by giving them context, more robust versioning, and a little bit of metadata.</a> <a data-t="407">So, you can kind of manage, you know, your production versus your staging environments. So, you make sure your models are well organized.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="412">MLOps is deploying.</a> <a data-t="416">And this is a loaded question in Slido we have for you.</a> <a data-t="418">Have you ever deployed a model? Deploying can mean different things to different people.</a> <a data-t="425">But it might have actually been something you've done before and didn't even realize it.</a> <a data-t="429">Whenever you're taking a model out of the environment that it's been trained in, and you're putting it in some other computational environment, you can say that is deployment.</a> <a data-t="439">So, things like putting a model in a </a><a href="/software/shiny-r/">Shiny</a><a data-t="439"> app are deploying.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="444">But we really believe that stable models are models that are in REST APIs.</a> <a data-t="452">So, this is an individual computational environment that just hosts your model at an API endpoint.</a> <a data-t="460">So, it is independent of maybe a larger application.</a> <a data-t="463">It makes it faster and easier and a little bit more robust to just host this model by itself and not inside of an application.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="478">And MLOps is monitoring.</a> <a data-t="480">This is tracking model performance.</a> <a data-t="482">So, if you are not monitoring your model, you might be immune to if your model is performing poorly.</a> <a data-t="490">And that's because models can fail silently.</a> <a data-t="493">It can be at 0% accuracy and still spitting out predictions left and right.</a> <a data-t="499">So, you really have to be tracking this model performance so you can tell when things are going wrong.</a></p>

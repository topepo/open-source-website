<h3>Finding packages</h3>

<p><a data-t="80">One way to keep up with all the things that are out there, I don't necessarily would advocate</a> <a data-t="84">for this to be the most efficient way to understand what's out there, but certainly</a> <a data-t="88">a fun way are all the different R blogs out there, and both R Weekly and R bloggers are</a> <a data-t="94">aggregator sites, and you can contribute your own blog to it by providing them with your</a> <a data-t="99">RSS feed.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="102">If you'd rather have search functionality, Rseek might be your thing.</a> <a data-t="107">That is a Google custom search which gives you results from R-related sites like the</a> <a data-t="111">R project site itself, GitHub, R bloggers, Stack Overflow and friends, and these kind</a> <a data-t="118">of sites.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="119">Cran-tastic is a community site for R packages where you can search, review, and tag Cran</a> <a data-t="125">packages, and on Cran itself are the task views.</a> <a data-t="130">They are in that menu on the left-hand side, but I've met quite a few people who are not</a> <a data-t="136">aware of them, so I thought I'd point that out.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="139">They provide you with information about which packages are available given a certain topic</a> <a data-t="145">like Bayesian inference, or natural language processing, or high-performance computing,</a> <a data-t="150">or web interfaces, or psychometrics, or whatever floats your boat.</a> <a data-t="156">These are usually curated lists where they group packages according to sub-topics.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="164">Curated lists are hard to keep up to date, so, if you spot something that you think should</a> <a data-t="169">be on there, or want to contribute in any other way, I think the best way is to email</a> <a data-t="174">the maintainer of the task view.</a> <a data-t="176">That should always be on the top with a name and an email address where you can contact</a> <a data-t="180">that person.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="182">Another site is Metacran which aggregates information from Cran and GitHub.</a> <a data-t="188">It also has search functionality and sections like featured packages, and trending packages,</a> <a data-t="194">most downloaded, most dependent upon.</a> <a data-t="197">So I think these kind of measures try to capture package popularity.</a></p>

<h3>Comparing and evaluating packages</h3>

<p><a data-t="203">That may be useful to discover what's out there, but also when you try to narrow down</a> <a data-t="207">your selection, however, Metacran doesn't give you direct functionality to look at these</a> <a data-t="213">numbers for your custom selection of packages.</a></p>

(40:26)\n\nStorytelling is critical. \n\nI go back to thinking about how we speak to little kids. You need to speak their language. They are not going to speak your language. If you spoke in Shakespearean language to a kid, they’re not going to understand you and they don’t care. You’re going to lose them.\n\nListen to your leaders.\n\nListen to the earning calls or whatever ways that they present to the organization. Pay attention to the words they use. Pay attention to how they describe things and the metrics they share. \n\nUse the words they use.\n\nWords are important. You can say the same thing with different words. We talk about data foundation, but if you say data integrity - you might say, well data integrity and data foundation are the same. If they use the word data foundation, use that. Use their words so they can understand you.\n\nKeep it consistent.\n\nThink about the old times - and the old fashioned newspaper. The New York Times always looks the same. They have the same structure, the same format. The articles and the layout are similar every day. \n\nImagine if they tweak things and move things around - you will spend a lot of energy and time trying to figure it out. It takes mental energy to figure out the format and the structure that you don’t care about. You want to read the news. All your energy is to understand the content of the news.\n\nThe same thing with storytelling and dashboards. Find a way to use the words, the structure, and the metrics that they use and are familiar with so that they don’t spend time and energy trying to understand your dashboard - your story.\n\nFor example: the metric that you’re tracking is going this way, we might need to take action on this. \n\nBe very specific.\n\nDon’t say a lot of things. Less is better. Be clear, concise, and very specific on the things that you really care about. \n\nRepeat the things you care about.\n\nSilence doesn’t mean they agree. Watch them, look at their expressions and repeat.\n\nA mistake that we can make is that we don’t recap what we talked about last month.

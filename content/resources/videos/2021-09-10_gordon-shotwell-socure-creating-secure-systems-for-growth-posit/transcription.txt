<h3>The economic case for buying software</h3>

<p><a data-t="2152">Okay, so the last thing I want to talk about was buying software,</a> <a data-t="2152">and I wanted to give you an economic argument about why buying software</a> <a data-t="2157">is always better from a security perspective.</a> <a data-t="2161">And this is an argument that you can use to get licensing money from your company.</a> <a data-t="2168">So at most companies, security is a cost center.</a> <a data-t="2168">What a cost center is something the company needs to spend money on,</a> <a data-t="2172">but doesn't make revenue based on in the short term.</a> <a data-t="2177">So like you need to spend money on an office space or you used to.</a> <a data-t="2177">You don't directly make money on that office, the office building, right?</a> <a data-t="2185">You make money on the stuff that happens in that office building.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2217">So every business or almost every business is going to go through rough times</a> <a data-t="2217">where they don't have enough cash flow.</a> <a data-t="2210">And in those times they were going to cut costs.</a> <a data-t="2230">They're going to neglect cost centers and they're going to put money into revenue centers.</a> <a data-t="2234">So the place that actually puts money on the balance sheet.</a> <a data-t="2239">And they'll do this even if it's in the long run, bad idea.</a> <a data-t="2244">Like even if it's a long run mistake to cut those cost centers, like cut those security people, cut those DevOps people.</a> <a data-t="2250">When you're kind of get in a threatening economic situation or threatening business situation, you just need to put revenue back on the business.</a> <a data-t="2254">You need to get it back into the black.</a> <a data-t="2254">So you'll tend to neglect cost centers.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2269">So while security is a cost for you, for the vendor, it is a revenue center.</a> <a data-t="2269">So if Databricks or RStudio is thinking about like, should I invest in wrapping this brand new awesome security</a> <a data-t="2276">protocol? If they do that, there's going to be some customer out there who's going to buy their</a> <a data-t="2281">product because they did that.</a> <a data-t="2281">So they're going to put money and stuff into that thing, even in times where their business is difficult.</a> <a data-t="2288">Because that's a big part of what the product is that they're selling.</a> <a data-t="2293">They're selling something that is secure, something that is compliant,</a> <a data-t="2296">something that works for all these companies that they're trying to sell to.</a> <a data-t="2302">So whenever you buy software or licensed software, you're basically moving security from living in your cost center</a> <a data-t="2311">world to being in the revenue center of your business.</a> <a data-t="2316">Because you're kind of like offshoring it to some degree.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2321">So a little bit, it's like a maintenance commitment to the future about we're going to keep this up to date.</a> <a data-t="2324">We're buying the software now, we're also committing to paying license fees for the next however long we use this thing.</a> <a data-t="2328">And that's kind of like putting a little bit of money every year into that system getting better and more secure.</a> <a data-t="2337">So this is really important also if you're growing, because as you're growing, your security requirements change.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1983">So a lot of data, tracking data at the major league level.</a> <a data-t="1988">We have these same systems at the minor league levels too.</a> <a data-t="1992">So if you're a minor league player for the Marlins, you're seeing the same technology footprint when you enter the minor leagues at our low A facility in Jupiter, Florida, all the way to the major leagues in Miami.</a> <a data-t="2006">So there's a consistent coaching and player development approach all the way from the minor leagues all the way up to the major leagues.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2015">So the biggest thing here is just that there's just a proliferation of more data, lots of problems in big data, but there's a lot more data being captured even for college players, for high school players, for international players that you just didn't have before.</a> <a data-t="2036">You're just used to traditional scouting approaches where people would travel all over the US and internationally looking at players and just kind of grading their talents on look and feel.</a> <a data-t="2051">But now we have data on all these players and we have models and projection systems that can be used.</a> <a data-t="2059">And I think that's the biggest change from previous Moneyball era.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2064">Yeah, do you feel like the qualitative is still in there though?</a> <a data-t="2067">Like now it's a blend of qualitative and quantitative, like is the look and feel still there from scouts?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2073">Certainly.</a> <a data-t="2074">And one of the things that we've been able to do here at the Marlins is use AI tooling to get sentiment analysis and evaluate kind of the traditional qualitative approach by our scouts.</a> <a data-t="2089">And our scouts are in the room when we are making decisions around who we draft and they're taking stands on certain players.</a> <a data-t="2097">And all of that goes into our projections and our models.</a> <a data-t="2102">So inevitably we take all those qualitative and quantitative inputs and that produces our overall Marlins sentiment on certain players.</a></p>

<h3>Innovation, differentiation, and feature engineering</h3>

<p><a data-t="2216">I think there are things that we're doing better than other teams and things that other teams are probably doing better than us.</a> <a data-t="2222">One of the things that I'm a part of at MLB is a central technology working group where MLB is democratizing certain technologies and platforms that all teams have access to</a> <a data-t="2237">because they're not necessarily competitive advantages relative.</a> <a data-t="2242">So all teams have access to generally the same data and infrastructure at just like a base level.</a> <a data-t="2249">And one of the reasons why that's really important is you don't want it to be an arms race where the teams that have the most money get access to the most data and the most insights.</a> <a data-t="2261">So there needs to be some standards and governance around that.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2271">Teams are building their own projection systems and their own models based on a lot of this data.</a> <a data-t="2279">But I think one of the differentiating factors for us and probably other teams where we can get more ingrained and more nuanced with our analytics is in the practice space actually and the non-game events.</a> <a data-t="2295">So our ability to simulate game-like conditions in practice and to track that using the same technology gets us a lot more data and insight.</a> <a data-t="2309">So we're really investing heavily in the wearables as I mentioned, having more game-like conditions in practice.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2318">And I think at the end of the day, coaching and player development is going to be one of those things that provides the differentiation.</a> <a data-t="2326">Players buying into what we're trying to do culturally is also going to be really important because if players aren't bought in, they're not going to want to try to implement the strategies.</a> <a data-t="2338">So there's a cultural aspect.</a> <a data-t="2339">There's a practice aspect.</a> <a data-t="2344">But at the end of the day, two teams are competing on roughly the same field with the same conditions and trying to beat the other team.</a> <a data-t="2352">And we're just trying to make the best decisions that we can to put the best product in the field.</a> <a data-t="2358">But I'm not the one hitting the baseball.</a> <a data-t="2363">You don't want me hitting the baseball.</a> <a data-t="2366">So people that are much better at this than me are doing that.</a> <a data-t="2370">And all that preparation, all that practice is going to be our differentiator.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2461">Yeah, one of the things that we've been really taking a look at here at the Marlins in a broad sense are what are the data points that best tell the stories about player performance?</a> <a data-t="2474">And there's all these stats, right?</a> <a data-t="2476">There's all these things that happen on the field and off the field.</a> <a data-t="2481">But eventually we build all these models and there's all these outputs, but effectively we just come to very sort of core numbers.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2495">And in baseball, it's effectively a couple of things.</a> <a data-t="2502">One, you've probably heard about this a lot.</a> <a data-t="2502">It's called wins above replacement and it's effectively MLB's calculation of how good is a player relative to just someone coming up from the minor leagues essentially.</a> <a data-t="2514">And so our ability to project that value into the future successfully gives us really good insights into how a player is going to perform.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2527">The other is a pretty widely adopted standard, but at the Marlins, we call it surplus value.</a> <a data-t="2537">And that is based on their projections and their contract and all those obligations.</a> <a data-t="2547">How much value does that player have to the team?</a> <a data-t="2554">Like just for like dollars and cents.</a> <a data-t="2555">So we just had the trade deadline last week and we made a few trades and we didn't make a few trades,</a> <a data-t="2564">but the key thing for us is, okay, if this player we think is worth, I don't know, 20 million dollars of surplus value, we essentially want to get players back that equal or surpass our surplus value calculations.</a> <a data-t="2582">And teams all calculate this differently.</a> <a data-t="2584">So there are market inefficiencies and information asymmetry that can mean that both sides can feel like they're ripping the other team off.</a> <a data-t="2595">But in actuality, both teams like the other team's players better.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2604">So there's a lot of nuance, but at the end of the day, we have come and sort of gotten consensus on what we feel the key data items are.</a> <a data-t="2613">And a lot of our R&D efforts are working towards how can we get as accurate as we can with those 10 to 20 data items.</a> <a data-t="2623">And that really guides the story for everything.</a> <a data-t="2626">And when we're building applications, it's how do we platform that data in a way that looks and feels right.</a> <a data-t="2634">And we're focusing a lot on data visualization and data design so that we can tell those stories with data really easily.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="2646">And the last thing we want to do is waste time, just as an example from the trade deadline, if I feel like one player is worth $20 million and Libby thinks that player's worth $25 million and Rachel feels like that player's worth $15 million,</a> <a data-t="2659">now we're sort of going back and forth on what we think the true value of the player is rather than this is what the Marlins think this player is worth.</a> <a data-t="2668">And that's what we're going to use as the basis because we know what has gone into those calculations that we all agree in the methodology that went into those calculations.</a> <a data-t="2679">And that was really important for us.</a> <a data-t="2681">This trade deadline was having consensus on what we thought the methodology was and trusting the modeling and the analytical results and using that as our source of truth.</a></p>

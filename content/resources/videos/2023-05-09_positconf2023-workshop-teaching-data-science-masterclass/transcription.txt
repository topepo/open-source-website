<h3>Three reasons to take this workshop</h3>

<p><a data-t="30">Here are the top three reasons why you should</a> <a data-t="35">take this workshop. First reason is keeping up with the evolving</a> <a data-t="40">data science ecosystem is not trivial. Keeping up with it and deciding whether it's the right</a> <a data-t="45">time to update your curriculum to incorporate new tools or new features of existing tools,</a> <a data-t="51">new data sources, or new workflows, and doing so while concurrently teaching can be overwhelming.</a> <a data-t="55">This workshop is designed to share with you what's new in the land of teaching data science</a> <a data-t="60">with R and to distill for you how you can implement these updates in your curricula.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="65">Second, successful teaching doesn't end with successful curriculum design. The computational</a> <a data-t="70">infrastructure you use for your teaching is an equally important part of a successful</a> <a data-t="75">data science course, particularly if your definition of success takes into consideration</a> <a data-t="80">the experience and enjoyment of your students as well as of yourself. In this workshop,</a> <a data-t="84">we'll talk quite a bit about how to set up computational teaching environments with Posit</a> <a data-t="89">Cloud as well as other server-based tools, how to use Git and GitHub for version control,</a> <a data-t="94">collaboration, and as your course management system, and how to teach reproducible authoring</a> <a data-t="99">with </a><a href="/software/quarto/">Quarto</a><a data-t="99"> as well as how to use it to build your course materials and sites.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="104">And third, this workshop will supercharge your learning and teaching prep. In one day</a> <a data-t="108">of the workshop, you will pick up a wealth of information and tips that can save you</a> <a data-t="112">weeks and months of trial and error on your own.

<p><a data-t="0">So hi, my name is Harvey Lieberman and I'm here today to tell you a little bit about</a> <a data-t="10">the R in Pharma organisation, who we are, what we do and how you can get involved if</a> <a data-t="16">you're interested.</a> <a data-t="25">So R is ubiquitous within the pharmaceutical industry, but until recently</a> <a data-t="25">there hasn't really been a group that works across multiple pharma companies to provide</a> <a data-t="31">a space for users to interact.</a> <a data-t="39">So in 2017, a group of R users from several pharma companies</a> <a data-t="39">got together with other organisations interested in this space, such as the FDA and Harvard</a> <a data-t="45">to form a group and that's how R Pharma was born.</a></p>

<h3>Goals and focus of R/Pharma</h3>

<p><a data-t="70">Now the goal of R Pharma has been to host a scientifically and industry oriented conference</a> <a data-t="79">focused on the use of R in the development of pharmaceuticals.</a> <a data-t="84">Conference covers topics</a> <a data-t="84">such as genomics, drug discovery, PKPD, clinical trials, regulatory compliance and validation,</a> <a data-t="93">as well as more general topics such as reproducible research and DevOps.</a> <a data-t="100">The focus is on the use</a> <a data-t="100">of R as a primary but not necessarily exclusive tool in the drug development process.</a> <a data-t="107">We also</a> <a data-t="107">hold workshops at the conference, but since the primary focus is for R practitioners,</a> <a data-t="114">the workshops are really aimed at those who are already competent in R.</a> <a data-t="120">Anyone's welcome</a> <a data-t="120">to present a workshop and we're lucky to have some really amazing presenters as you'll see</a> <a data-t="124">on some of the later slides.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="134">Now, there are plenty of data science conferences, workshops</a> <a data-t="134">and even organisations.</a> <a data-t="142">So what makes us different?</a> <a data-t="142">Well, for one, we're run by Pharma</a> <a data-t="142">for Pharma.</a> <a data-t="148">We concentrate on R, which is not to say we don't have contributing talks</a> <a data-t="148">that include Python, SAS or even Julia, but just that R is the main focus.</a> <a data-t="156">The R in Pharma</a> <a data-t="156">conference that we've held the past three years has been free.</a> <a data-t="163">Any cost has been assumed</a> <a data-t="163">by an advisory group, which is generally made up of Pharma companies willing to support</a> <a data-t="169">the cross fertilisation that occurs when you put a bunch of curious programmers together.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="174">In a similar vein, we have no sponsors and nor do we allow sponsorship at the conference</a> <a data-t="182">or any events because we believe that community led effort works best when there's no outside</a> <a data-t="189">influence.</a> <a data-t="196">We're also focused on diversity and have goals on providing travel grants</a> <a data-t="196">to those who need assistance in attending the meetings.</a></p>

<h3>Conference history: 2018–2020</h3>

<p><a data-t="203">So why am I here today?</a> <a data-t="203">Well,</a> <a data-t="203">I hope you'll understand that the RFOMA is a very open organisation made up of volunteers.</a> <a data-t="209">We'd welcome anyone wishing to get involved from presenting at a meeting to reaching out</a> <a data-t="213">to the community with a question and more on how you can get involved in 2021 a little</a> <a data-t="218">later.</a> <a data-t="224">But first, I'd like to share some information on how we've evolved since 2018.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="233">RFOMA holds a conference each year.</a> <a data-t="233">The first was held in 2018 at Harvard.</a> <a data-t="239">And if you're</a> <a data-t="239">unable to attend, many of the presentations are available on the RFOMA website.</a> <a data-t="246">The links</a> <a data-t="246">on this slide and I invite you to peruse them.</a> <a data-t="252">We had around 150 attendees, which was limited</a> <a data-t="252">by the size of the venue, and it ran over two days.</a> <a data-t="258">We're lucky to have some really</a> <a data-t="258">amazing keynotes that are listed here.</a> <a data-t="264">There are a number of amazing general presentations</a> <a data-t="264">that range from RNA sequencing to how to convert statisticians to data scientists to using</a> <a data-t="266">our energy XP environment.</a> <a data-t="275">One thing that was clear from 2018 was that </a><a href="/software/shiny-r/">Shiny</a><a data-t="275"> was the</a> <a data-t="275">main focus of most presenters work.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="286">In 2019, we repeated the conference, this time expanding</a> <a data-t="286">to longer workshops each half a day.</a> <a data-t="291">Once again, the conference was held at Harvard,</a> <a data-t="291">with a mixture of keynotes and general presentations for around 150 attendees.</a> <a data-t="296">I'd really like</a> <a data-t="296">to highlight the workshops here.</a> <a data-t="302">We reached out to a number of workshop presenters, all</a> <a data-t="302">of whom agreed to put together a half day workshop at no cost.</a> <a data-t="308">And all the workshop</a> <a data-t="308">materials and many of the presentations are available through the RFOMA website.</a> <a data-t="314">What</a> <a data-t="314">was interesting in 2019, there was still a focus on Shiny, but there was a huge focus</a> <a data-t="320">on reproducibility.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="330">So, like many other groups in 2020, early on, we decided to switch to</a> <a data-t="330">a fully virtual conference.</a> <a data-t="337">This provided few benefits over historical in-person meeting.</a> <a data-t="345">First, we could reach more attendees and we received over 500 registrations from 26 countries.</a> <a data-t="352">We were expecting around 300 and initially had prepared for up to 500.</a> <a data-t="359">But as soon as</a> <a data-t="359">it opened, it was clear that we were going to hit 1,000 plus.</a> <a data-t="365">And the graph on the right</a> <a data-t="365">is actually really interesting.</a> <a data-t="365">It shows the registration.</a> <a data-t="371">This was registration without</a> <a data-t="371">advertising.</a> <a data-t="377">So, just through word of mouth, it's amazing how quickly we had registration.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="378">Secondly, we could attract more presenters because people didn't have to travel.</a> <a data-t="384">There</a> <a data-t="384">were more people willing to present.</a> <a data-t="390">In fact, we had over 200 proposals for presentations.</a> <a data-t="390">Conference was held over three days, each four hours long, running in the morning for</a> <a data-t="397">those on the West Coast of the US and the evenings in Europe.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="397">So, I also want to highlight</a> <a data-t="402">the workshops that were held for the 2020 conference.</a> <a data-t="407">Because of the virtual nature,</a> <a data-t="407">we could organize a little bit differently and stretched out over multiple days.</a> <a data-t="413">We ran</a> <a data-t="413">a total of 11 workshops through the week before the conference, each three to four hours long.</a> <a data-t="417">There's some incredible presenters who put a lot of time and effort into building them.</a> <a data-t="424">And all the environments were pre-configured under </a><a href="/software/rstudio/">RStudio</a><a data-t="424"> Cloud.</a> <a data-t="430">So, it was very easy</a> <a data-t="430">for people to come in, use the materials and access them afterwards.</a> <a data-t="437">Once again, these</a> <a data-t="437">were all put on by the presenters at no charge.</a> <a data-t="443">And once again, the conference is free anyway.</a> <a data-t="449">So, it was a really amazing piece of work.</a></p>

<h3>Looking ahead to 2021</h3>

<p><a data-t="457">Our pharma meeting for 2021 is almost certainly</a> <a data-t="457">going to be a fully virtual event.</a> <a data-t="463">And thinking about the trends we've seen over the past</a> <a data-t="463">three years, R is definitely growing within the pharma and biotech industries at quite</a> <a data-t="469">a rate.</a> <a data-t="474">We now have more mature infrastructures in place, and there's definitely a move towards</a> <a data-t="474">more submissions using R.</a> <a data-t="480">In addition, there's greater work in combining languages, such</a> <a data-t="480">as R and Python and R and SAS.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="477">I want to switch gears just a little bit and talk about something</a> <a data-t="486">new.</a> <a data-t="486">We'll be launching a learning and development platform this year called LearnRinPharma.com.</a> <a data-t="497">And the goal of this is to help pharma professionals develop the skills needed in the drug development</a> <a data-t="503">space.</a> <a data-t="503">The RINPharma group will be helping community members put on workshops, lectures,</a> <a data-t="509">teaching labs in cloud environments with the aim of building practical R skills in a friendly</a> <a data-t="515">environment.</a> <a data-t="510">At a very high level, what we're hoping to do is to put together a monthly</a> <a data-t="516">write-up on a topic and then follow it by a lecture or workshop, even a workflow, maybe</a> <a data-t="522">a community discussion.</a> <a data-t="528">This is one way that you, the pharma community, can contribute.</a> <a data-t="528">If you'd like to lead a discussion group, teach a lab, please get in touch with us.</a> <a data-t="532">Contact details will be on the final slide.</a> <a data-t="532">And keep an eye out for LearnRinPharma.com.</a> <a data-t="538">We'll announce more through a Twitter feed and the RINPharma website.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="541">The first RINPharma conference that we held three years ago was dominated by Shiny apps.</a> <a data-t="547">We can see how Shiny can play a role in modernizing the drug submission process, but it's fraught</a> <a data-t="554">with challenges.</a> <a data-t="554">These can be related to technology, regulatory agencies, training</a> <a data-t="561">and validation, qualification, and even people and culture, plus others.</a> <a data-t="572">In 2021, our pharma</a> <a data-t="572">wants to tackle Shiny for submissions as part of LearnRinPharma.com.</a></p>

<h3>tidymodels performance</h3>

<p><a data-t="1208">One other question was, how performant or fast is tidymodels compared to other frameworks in R?</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1220">So tidymodels doesn't implement the algorithms used to train the models ourselves.</a> <a data-t="1224">We make use of existing packages in R to make that happen.</a> <a data-t="1229">And then we have a layer on top that sort of standardizes the way that you interface with those models.</a> <a data-t="1237">Earlier this year, we spent a good amount of time making sure that the layer on top of those existing R packages that we've written is as performant as possible.</a> <a data-t="1250">So once you're training more complex models or you're working with data that most people will see in an applied context, that overhead is basically negligible.</a> <a data-t="1255">So your modeling pipeline is as fast as is otherwise possible in R.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="1262">We also have a few tricks up our sleeves to reduce the amount of models that you actually need to train to still be able to evaluate many models at once.</a> <a data-t="1274">And we try to make it very easy to accidentally do the most performant approach to a modeling problem that somebody might be tackling.</a></p>

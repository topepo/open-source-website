<h3>Three-step implementation approach</h3>

<p><a data-t="296">Well, at Covestro, we did it in a three-step way.</a> <a data-t="300">This is actually our good practice of how to introduce centralized data science infrastructures.</a> <a data-t="307">So the first step is an assessment step, where we try to find out, okay, where are you?</a> <a data-t="315">What's your position at the moment?</a> <a data-t="316">So what are the databases that you could never get rid of, for example?</a> <a data-t="321">And what are the technologies where you say, well, they are not up-to-date anymore.</a> <a data-t="327">And it might be possible to migrate.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="331">And we also want to know, where are you now in terms of data science use cases?</a> <a data-t="337">And where are you seeing yourself in the foreseeable future, in the next two, three years or so?</a> <a data-t="345">And yeah, one of the central aspects of those assessments is to bring all the perspectives,</a> <a data-t="350">the business perspective, the data governance perspective, the IT operation perspective,</a> <a data-t="354">and the data science perspective all together.</a> <a data-t="359">And this is really important to have from every stakeholder group, somebody on the table</a> <a data-t="365">all at once, right?</a> <a data-t="366">So that there's a common understanding where we want to go and how to do it.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="368">The next step is the implementation itself.</a> <a data-t="373">It means just setting up the infrastructure where we have just decided for.</a> <a data-t="379">And it's, yeah, the implementation step, from our point of view, is really like you would</a> <a data-t="385">do it in a data science project.</a> <a data-t="387">You think of iterations.</a> <a data-t="389">You start with a minimal viable product where somebody can use it and where you can gain</a> <a data-t="396">the experiences from the users, from the data scientists, in order to improve the infrastructure.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="403">And finally, there's the operation side.</a> <a data-t="407">So once everything is in place and the business and data science people are kind of running</a> <a data-t="413">crucial apps on this infrastructure, it needs to be operated.</a> <a data-t="419">And then there's like a step forward, step back.</a> <a data-t="422">It's like DevOps idea, implement and operate more or less at the same time.</a></p>

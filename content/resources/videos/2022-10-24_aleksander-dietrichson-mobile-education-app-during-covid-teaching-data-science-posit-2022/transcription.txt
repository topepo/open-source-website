<h3>Context: COVID and emergency online teaching</h3>

<p><a data-t="66">And the story here is as</a> <a data-t="66">we all know there was a pandemic in Argentina.</a> <a data-t="71">We went into full lockdown in</a> <a data-t="71">March of 2020 and then that meant that all instructions needed to be online in</a> <a data-t="78">a hurry.</a> <a data-t="82">Since we're in the southern hemisphere this actually coincided</a> <a data-t="82">pretty much with the beginning of the semester so we didn't have to do it mid</a> <a data-t="85">semester.</a> <a data-t="92">But what happened is that everyone started to teach using Zoom or</a> <a data-t="92">any kind of video conferencing system that was available and we found that</a> <a data-t="99">less than half the students actually had access to a laptop.</a> <a data-t="104">So in practice they</a> <a data-t="104">were going to follow the course on a device, on an Android phone typically.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="111">And so what we wanted to do, and this is something that we worked on before, is to</a> <a data-t="123">help them create, help the instructors create a feedback loop.</a> <a data-t="127">Because as Kelly</a> <a data-t="127">mentioned in her talk there is a lot of nonverbal communication that goes on</a> <a data-t="132">when you actually meet face-to-face.</a> <a data-t="136">That's part of the reason we're all here.</a> <a data-t="142">And you lose that, you lose some of it at least when you do video conferencing.</a> <a data-t="145">Especially if the class is a big class with a hundred students or something</a> <a data-t="145">like that.</a> <a data-t="150">So what we set out to do was to mitigate some of this by creating a</a> <a data-t="150">tool to to provide feedback so the students could provide feedback to the</a> <a data-t="157">instructor.</a></p>

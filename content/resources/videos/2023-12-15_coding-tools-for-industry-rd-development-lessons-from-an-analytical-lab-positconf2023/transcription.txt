<h3>Be a detective</h3>

<p><a data-t="146">Your first assignment is to be a detective.</a> <a data-t="149">Shadow your users to discover their sources of pain points and inefficiencies.</a> <a data-t="154">By asking questions as they load samples, run measurements, and analyze data, you will</a> <a data-t="160">identify clues and assumptions that are overlooked when these steps are simply described to you.</a> <a data-t="165">For example, you notice that your users can export data in a different format that is</a> <a data-t="170">easier to parse, or that it's possible to save time and avoid copy-pasting errors by</a> <a data-t="175">extracting instrument metadata from a file otherwise ignored.</a></p>

<h3>Be an architect</h3>

<p><a data-t="180">Next, be an architect.</a> <a data-t="184">Present your discoveries to the team on the drawing board, help them create the big picture,</a> <a data-t="188">and map the path forward.</a> <a data-t="190">Brace yourself to negotiate structural and aesthetic preferences and balance time frame,</a> <a data-t="196">cost, and risk constraints.</a> <a data-t="198">Another potential challenge is getting users on board with the solutions they need instead</a> <a data-t="203">of replicating their favorite software features.</a></p>

<p><a data-t="206">Maybe your users are fixated on how they like to edit and annotate graphs in Excel because</a> <a data-t="211">they haven't grasped the benefits of reproducible and interactive plots yet.</a> <a data-t="217">Maybe you've been tasked with building a web app, but you've identified that what they</a> <a data-t="221">need is an automated and scheduled report.</a> <a data-t="224">My point is, here's your chance to pitch your approach and influence the foundation of the</a> <a data-t="229">project in a rewarding way.</a></p>
